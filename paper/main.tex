\title{EVQ-Cosh: Variational Frequency Allocation for Rotary Position Embedding}

\author{Anonymous Authors}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Every mainstream RoPE implementation distributes frequencies across channels using the same geometric (equal-ratio) schedule introduced by RoFormer, yet this allocation is a degenerate limiting case---the $\tau{=}0$ boundary of a broader variational family---not a structurally distinguished optimum.
We formulate channel-frequency allocation as a variational problem, derive a closed-form one-parameter family (EVQ-cosh) whose $\tau{=}0$ limit recovers geometric RoPE, and show that a deliberately simple, semi-analytic temperature rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$---sub-optimal by construction---already matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations without any learned parameters.
The strongest systems result is compositional: on MHA (3~seeds), EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ passkey retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ where Geo+YaRN plateaus at $61\%$, with $14$--$32\%$ PPL reductions at $8$--$16\mathrm{K}$.
On MLA (3~seeds), where only 16 RoPE channels remain, EVQ \emph{alone} outperforms Geo+YaRN at $2\times$ extrapolation ($-31.1\%$ PPL).
In PE-dominant extreme extrapolation, EVQ surpasses learnable positional encoding with zero extra parameters ($333.7$ vs.\ $455.3$ PPL).
These results establish frequency allocation as a previously neglected and increasingly consequential RoPE design axis: the structural correction matters more than the precision of the rule that selects it.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Broader Impact}

This work studies frequency allocation as a design axis for rotary position embedding rather than a specific deployed application. Its main positive impact is scientific and systems-oriented: replacing the default geometric frequency schedule with a principled variational allocation can reduce wasted compute and improve the reliability of long-context models across architectures. The main risk is over-generalization. Our strongest evidence remains concentrated on text extrapolation and retrieval-heavy long-context evaluation, so we do not present EVQ as a universal guarantee of downstream improvement across all tasks, modalities, or deployment settings.
