\title{EVQ-Cosh: Variational Frequency Allocation for Rotary Position Embedding}

\author{Anonymous Authors}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Channel-frequency allocation---how RoPE distributes frequencies across head dimensions---has been fixed at the geometric (equal-ratio) schedule since RoFormer. We show this schedule is not a generic optimum but the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit of a variational family, and identify allocation as a consequential third RoPE design axis alongside base frequency and head dimension.
We derive a closed-form one-parameter family (EVQ-cosh) and a zero-parameter temperature rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ that is deliberately sub-optimal yet matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations---evidence that the frequency allocation landscape contains a wide basin around the variational solution.
A zero-parameter closed-form initialization outperforms a 32-parameter learned PE ($333.7$ vs.\ $455.3$ PPL) and reduces Geo baseline by $35\%$ in extreme extrapolation, composes with YaRN to unlock $100\%$ passkey retrieval where Geo+YaRN plateaus at $61\%$ (3~seeds), and on MLA with only 16 RoPE channels, alone reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL by $31.1\%$ relative to Geo baseline---surpassing Geo+YaRN at that length.
As architectures compress positional information into fewer channels, the structural correction---redistributing frequency mass from redundant high-frequency channels to the information-starved low-frequency regime---becomes particularly decisive.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Broader Impact}

This work studies frequency allocation as a design axis for rotary position embedding rather than a specific deployed application. Its main positive impact is scientific and systems-oriented: replacing the default geometric frequency schedule with a principled variational allocation can reduce wasted compute and improve the reliability of long-context models across architectures. The main risk is over-generalization. Our strongest evidence remains concentrated on text extrapolation and retrieval-heavy long-context evaluation, so we do not present EVQ as a universal guarantee of downstream improvement across all tasks, modalities, or deployment settings.
