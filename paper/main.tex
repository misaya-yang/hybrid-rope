\title{EVQ-Cosh: Closed-Form Frequency Allocation for Long-Context Extrapolation}

\author{Anonymous Authors}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Rotary position embedding is often extended at inference time, but its training-time geometric frequency allocation is usually left untouched. We formulate RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem, derive a broadband surrogate whose stationary density satisfies a second-order ODE, and obtain a closed-form family, EVQ-cosh, in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. Empirically, EVQ beats learnable adaptive positional encoding in DAPE-style extreme extrapolation (\(128\!\rightarrow\!8\mathrm{K}\): \(333.7\) vs.\ \(455.3\) PPL), showing that positional structure alone can determine extreme long-range behavior. More importantly, EVQ unlocks inference-time scaling: across six seeds, EVQ+YaRN reaches \(100\%\) retrieval at \(8\mathrm{K}\), while Geo+YaRN remains at \(61\%\) to \(65\%\); in a fair \(10\%\) mix comparison, EVQ+YaRN lowers PPL from \(82.9\) to \(70.9\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) and from \(157.7\) to \(107.5\) at \(16\mathrm{K}\). Under progressive training (\(512\!\rightarrow\!1024\!\rightarrow\!2048\)), this advantage widens monotonically as geometric RoPE degrades while EVQ remains stable, extending functional context to \(48\mathrm{K}\) (\(24\times\) training length) at PPL~\(2.63\). These results identify training-time frequency allocation as an under-explored but critical design axis for long-context extrapolation.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Broader Impact}

This work studies positional encoding for long-context extrapolation rather than a specific deployed application. Its main positive impact is scientific and systems-oriented: replacing ad hoc context-extension heuristics with a more principled training-time frequency design can reduce wasted compute and improve the reliability of long-context models. The main risk is over-generalization. Our strongest evidence remains concentrated on text extrapolation and retrieval-heavy long-context evaluation, so we do not present EVQ as a universal guarantee of downstream improvement across all tasks, modalities, or deployment settings.
