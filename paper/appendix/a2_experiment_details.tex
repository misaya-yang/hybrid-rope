The body centers three empirical anchors:
\begin{enumerate}
\item the EVQ\(\times\)YaRN systems result on passkey-mix data,
\item DAPE-style PE-dominant extrapolation at \(128\) tokens,

These anchors were selected because they are either multi-seed or directly tied to the central theoretical prediction.

\subsection{Larger-scale supporting evidence}
\label{sec:750m-supporting}

The \(750\mathrm{M}\) `continue@4K' experiment is reported as supporting evidence only. The reason is not that the result is weak; on the contrary, it shows \(16\mathrm{K}\) PPL improving from \(45.136\) to \(24.407\) and \(8\mathrm{K}\) AR exact improving from \(0\%\) to \(77.5\%\). The reason is evidence hygiene: the run is single-seed, and the downstream LongBench comparison is incomplete because the remote dataset download failed during evaluation.

This distinction is important for interpretation. The larger-scale run is best viewed as evidence that the same EVQ pattern persists after continued pretraining at a longer training length, not as the main fairness anchor of the paper. The main fairness anchors remain the multi-seed \(350\mathrm{M}\) and \(454\mathrm{M}\) comparisons used in the main text.

\subsection{Evaluation notes}

The paper uses three increasingly strict long-context evaluation views.
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{PPL}: global language-modeling quality under extrapolation.
\item \textbf{Retrieval}: whether the relevant item is still localized at long range.
\item \textbf{AR exact / multi-needle}: whether the localized information remains precisely recoverable.
\end{enumerate}

This split is necessary because stronger models can saturate coarse retrieval while still differing sharply in exact long-range recovery. The \(750\mathrm{M}\) supporting run is the clearest example: retrieval is already saturated, but AR exact separates geometric RoPE from EVQ very strongly.

\subsection{Cross-modal evidence: video temporal extrapolation}

Video temporal extrapolation tests whether the same frequency-allocation principle transfers beyond text RoPE. We apply EVQ-Cosh to the temporal channels of a 3D RoPE VideoGPT (268.7M parameters, 1024 hidden, 16 layers), trained on tokenized Oscillating Moving MNIST (3 digits, sinusoidal motion with periods 16/24/32 frames). Models train on 32-frame sequences and extrapolate to 128 frames (4$\times$) with temporal YaRN scaling.

\paragraph{Setup.} We compare \texttt{geo\_k16} (16 temporal channel pairs, geometric allocation, $\tau{=}0$) against \texttt{evq\_k16} (same budget, EVQ-Cosh with $\tau{=}K_t/\sqrt{T_\mathrm{train}}$). Both allocations share identical spatial channels. Models are evaluated on PPL, teacher-forced top-1 accuracy, and generation quality via Fr\'echet Video Distance (FVD). For FVD, we report both I3D features and VideoMAE-v2 features; the latter are approximately $3\times$ more sensitive to temporal distortion than I3D~\citep{ge2024contentbiasfvd}.

\paragraph{Results.} Table~\ref{tab:video-temporal} summarizes the findings. EVQ wins every metric, but the margins attenuate sharply from distributional quality (PPL) through generation:

The $3.2\times$ sensitivity ratio between VideoMAE and I3D FVD is consistent with the finding that I3D features are dominated by per-frame spatial content and nearly blind to temporal structure~\citep{ge2024contentbiasfvd}. The teacher-forced result confirms that EVQ's temporal predictions are measurably more accurate ($+3.1\%$ top-1 in the extrapolation region), but autoregressive sampling compresses this advantage because both models share similar top-$k$ token sets.

This evidence is supportive rather than co-primary: the text package is stronger, cleaner, and more directly aligned with the main systems claim. Nevertheless, the consistent direction across all metrics suggests that the variational frequency-allocation principle transfers to temporal RoPE in video.

\paragraph{Teacher-forced temporal precision.}
To isolate position-encoding quality from autoregressive error accumulation, we evaluate the same checkpoints in teacher-forced mode on 128-frame sequences (4$\times$ extrapolation with YaRN). Table~\ref{tab:temporal-precision} reports per-region top-1 and top-5 accuracy. In the training region (frames 0--31), both models are tied to within $0.06\%$, confirming matched capacity. In the extrapolation region, EVQ leads by $+3.14\%$ top-1 and $+5.40\%$ top-5. The advantage peaks in near-extrapolation (32--63f): $+3.74\%$ top-1, $+6.59\%$ top-5.

\paragraph{Frequency-resolved decomposition.}
Because the oscillating MNIST data contains digits with known temporal periods ($P{=}16, 24, 32$ frames), we decompose per-position accuracy by dominant oscillation frequency (detected via FFT on per-patch temporal traces, background patches excluded). Table~\ref{tab:freq-decomp} shows a monotonic frequency gradient: the EVQ advantage scales with temporal frequency, from $+3.55\%$ top-1 at $P{=}32$ (lowest frequency) to $+5.07\%$ at $P{=}16$ (highest frequency). Top-5 accuracy amplifies the pattern further, reaching $+10.39\%$ in the near-extrapolation zone at $P{=}16$.

This gradient directly validates the theoretical prediction. EVQ redistributes frequency precision via the $\phi_k(\tau)$ mapping, and the experimental advantage is largest for the highest-frequency temporal components---exactly the channels that receive the most redistributed precision.
