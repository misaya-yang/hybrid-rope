\paragraph{MLA: allocation matters more when channels are scarce.}
EVQ extends to MLA \citep{deepseekv2, deepseekv3}, where only 16 frequency channels carry positional information. On a 432M model (3-seed, 500M tokens, $L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}8192$), EVQ alone reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL by $31.1\%$ and outperforms Geo+YaRN at $16\mathrm{K}$. Preliminary evidence under thorough training (1B tokens, single seed) suggests that EVQ's raw advantage reverses ($+11.1\%$) while EVQ+YaRN after fine-tuning still wins ($-2.5\%$)---a $13.6\mathrm{pp}$ swing consistent with the frequency substrate remaining the binding constraint even when raw extrapolation saturates. Multi-seed replication is in progress. Full 3-seed standalone results are in Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}.

\paragraph{Post-training injection via LoRA (7B scale).}
As supporting post-hoc evidence, we inject EVQ-Cosh frequencies ($\tau{=}1.414$, the $\tau^*$ predicted for $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}128$, $L{=}8192$) into LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct \citep{grattafiori2024llama3} and fine-tune with LoRA ($r{=}64{=}K$, $\alpha{=}128$) on LongAlign-10k for 300 steps.

Table~\ref{tab:lora-8b} shows that EVQ-LoRA reduces extrapolation PPL by $8\times$ at $16\mathrm{K}$ and $19\times$ at $32\mathrm{K}$ with a modest in-distribution cost ($+30\%$ PPL at $8\mathrm{K}$), serving as supporting post-hoc evidence at 7B scale. The rank is set at $r{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/2{=}64$. The phenomenological variational model in \S\ref{sec:lora-regime} predicts a rank-dependent viability threshold near $r \approx d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$ under our calibration: below the threshold, coupling stiffness appears to prevent frequency redistribution; above it, extrapolation recovers. RULER scores do not improve, consistent with LoRA capacity sufficing for positional adaptation but not instruction-following at extrapolated lengths.

\paragraph{Progressive training (single seed, preliminary).}
Three-stage progressive training (454M, $512{\to}1024{\to}2048$) is reported as supporting evidence.
The EVQ+YaRN PPL gap at $16\mathrm{K}$ widens from $-34.6\%$ to $-81.2\%$, driven by Geo+YaRN degradation while EVQ+YaRN remains stable. At Stage~3, functional context reaches $48\mathrm{K}$ ($24\times$ training length) at PPL~$2.63$. After Stage~2, EVQ \emph{raw} (PPL $11.2$ at $16\mathrm{K}$) surpasses EVQ+YaRN at Stage~1 ($16.8$), suggesting that continued training can partially internalize the allocation benefit. This is a single-seed observation; multi-seed replication is in progress.

\paragraph{Cross-modal confirmation: video DiT (supporting).}
In 129.6M DiT head-to-head experiments (2~seeds, shared training run), EVQ wins every metric in both seeds, eliminating optimizer and CUDA confounds. Once the dead-channel bottleneck is removed (base-1000), both $\tau{=}1.2$ and $\tau{=}1.5$ beat Geo by ${\approx}48\%$ on far-frame MSE. This is a supporting mechanism check rather than a standalone video contribution: the collision bottleneck identified by the theory appears across modalities. Full results are in \S\ref{sec:tau-correction}.

\paragraph{Signal gradient across abstraction layers.}
Across the diagnostics reported here, EVQ's effect generally attenuates through successive abstraction layers: raw PPL $-46\%$ (Table~\ref{tab:multiscale}) $\to$ Gold-Answer NLL $-30\%$ (Table~\ref{tab:quality-nll}) $\to$ passkey $+59\mathrm{pp}$ vs.\ Geo baseline (EVQ+YaRN $100\%$ vs.\ Geo $41\%$, 3~seeds) $\to$ QA accuracy $+2.2\mathrm{pp}$ (Table~\ref{tab:quality-nll}, single seed, near-random baseline). This pattern is consistent with the intervention being largest at the PE layer and attenuated through higher abstraction where model capacity becomes the binding constraint.

\paragraph{Capability preservation.}
Table~\ref{tab:capability} shows that EVQ is non-destructive when retrieval is absent from training and improves extrapolated retrieval by $+10$--$13\mathrm{pp}$ when retrieval is learned (454M, 3-seed).
