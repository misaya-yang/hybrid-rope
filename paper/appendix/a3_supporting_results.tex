\section{Supporting Results}

\subsection{Multi-head Latent Attention}
\label{sec:mla-results}

To test whether EVQ's benefit extends to Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA), the compressed-RoPE attention variant introduced in DeepSeek-V2 \citep{deepseekv2}, we train a 432M-parameter MLA Transformer from scratch. Our configuration uses \(d_{\mathrm{rope}}{=}32\) and base${}=500\mathrm{K}$; production DeepSeek-V2/V3 \citep{deepseekv3} uses \(d_{\mathrm{rope}}{=}64\) and base${}=10\mathrm{K}$. The shared architectural property---a compressed RoPE subspace with far fewer frequency channels than standard MHA---is what makes allocation quality decisive. Only 16 frequency channels carry positional information, while the remaining \(\mathrm{kv\_rank}{=}256\) dimensions carry content. ($d_{\mathrm{rope}}$ in MLA notation is the same quantity as $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ in \S\ref{sec:theory}; we use the DeepSeek-style $d_{\mathrm{rope}}$ throughout this subsection.)

The model trains on FineWeb-Edu (500M tokens) at \(L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}8192\) with \(b{=}500\mathrm{K}\) across three seeds (42, 43, 88). EVQ uses \(\tau{=}1.414\) (i.e., \(d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L} = 128/\sqrt{8192}\)).

\paragraph{Two distinct dimensions.} It is important to distinguish two roles played by the head dimension. (i)~The \emph{kernel normalization} $\alpha{=}1/(2K){=}1/d_{\mathrm{rope}}$ controls the diagonal coefficient of $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ and depends only on the number of RoPE frequency pairs ($K{=}d_{\mathrm{rope}}/2$); for MLA this gives $\alpha{=}1/32$. (ii)~The \emph{operating-point dimension} $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ in $\tau^*{=}d_{\mathrm{eff}}/\sqrt{L}$ enters through the post-softmax transport utility $U$, which weights all attention-logit components carried by the head---including the non-RoPE content channels mixed in via the latent projection. The variational $\alpha{=}1/d_{\mathrm{rope}}$ derivation alone would give a much smaller deployment $\tau$; we deploy $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}128$ as a calibrated convention reflecting the latent-projection coupling, validated empirically by Table~\ref{tab:mla} but not derived from the broadband surrogate. A direct $\tau{=}d_{\mathrm{rope}}/\sqrt{L}$ ablation is the natural sanity check.

Table~\ref{tab:mla} shows the results. Three findings stand out. First, EVQ alone reduces PPL at \(16\mathrm{K}\) by \(31.1\%\) (\(138.8 \to 95.6\)) with only \(+1.1\%\) in-distribution cost---a larger relative gain than observed with standard MHA at comparable scales, consistent with the ``fewer channels, each more precious'' hypothesis. Second, EVQ alone outperforms GEO+YaRN(s=4) at \(16\mathrm{K}\) (\(-31.1\%\) vs \(-15.1\%\)); at \(24\mathrm{K}\) the two are tied within printed precision ($204.9$ each). Under matched YaRN scale in this setup, a training-time allocation change is thus comparable to or better than the inference-time rescaling baseline we tested, but we do not claim superiority over every possible inference-time method. Third, EVQ+YaRN is the best at every tested length; the ranking EVQ+YaRN $>$ EVQ $>$ GEO+YaRN $>$ GEO holds strictly at $16$K, with YaRN providing larger marginal benefit on EVQ (\(-25.6\%\)) than on GEO (\(-15.1\%\)) at \(16\mathrm{K}\), consistent with complementary mechanisms.

Training-progression analysis shows EVQ's advantage is visible from early training and grows monotonically to \(-31.1\%\) at completion, while the in-distribution cost simultaneously decreases from \(+1.4\%\) to \(+1.1\%\). This pattern is consistent with the benefit being an intrinsic property of the frequency allocation rather than a training-dynamics artifact.

\subsection{Downstream QA evaluation: QuALITY Gold NLL}
% \label{sec:quality-downstream} % unused

The body reports a QuALITY \citep{pang2022quality} QA evaluation on 454M finetuned checkpoints (\(n{=}2086\), distractor-padded, random article position 20\%--80\%). Table~\ref{tab:quality-nll} gives the full results. At this model scale, 4-option accuracy hovers near the 25\% random baseline for all configurations, consistent with 454M capacity---not positional encoding---being the binding constraint on downstream accuracy. However, gold-answer NLL separates the two RoPE variants: EVQ's NLL advantage grows from \(-1.7\%\) at \(4\mathrm{K}\) (in-distribution) to \(-30.1\%\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) (\(2\times\) extrapolation) and \(-21.4\%\) at \(16\mathrm{K}\) (\(4\times\)). YaRN at \(8\mathrm{K}\) recovers Geo's accuracy to \(4\mathrm{K}\) level but does not close the NLL gap (\(-8.1\%\)).

The key takeaway is that accuracy at the capacity floor is not a useful discriminator, but NLL on the correct answer preserves the signal. This is consistent with the PPL and passkey evidence in the body: EVQ improves the model's probabilistic calibration at extrapolated lengths even when accuracy is saturated by capacity constraints.
