\section{Conclusion}

We cast RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem and obtain a closed-form family in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. This reframes long-context extrapolation as a training-time frequency-allocation problem rather than an inference-only scaling problem.

Empirically, EVQ not only improves extreme extrapolation in PE-dominant regimes, but more importantly unlocks inference-time scaling: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN on long-context extrapolation. The central practical lesson is that long-context systems do not benefit only from better inference-time scaling rules; they also depend on a better training-time frequency substrate.
