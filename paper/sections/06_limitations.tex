\section{Limitations and Scope}

The theory has one explicit approximation: the broadband surrogate. Accordingly, the surrogate-to-ODE derivation is exact only conditioned on that surrogate, and the scaling law \(\tau^*(L)=d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}\) remains an empirical law / conjecture rather than an unconditional theorem.

Empirically, the primary evidence is still multi-seed text extrapolation. The \(750\mathrm{M}\) continue result is single-seed supporting evidence, and its broader downstream comparison is incomplete because of a dataset-download failure. The video temporal results (Tables~\ref{tab:video-temporal}--\ref{tab:freq-decomp}) are supportive rather than co-primary: teacher-forced evaluation shows $+5.4\%$ top-5 accuracy in the extrapolation zone and a monotonic frequency gradient matching the theoretical prediction ($+10.4\%$ at $P{=}16$, $+6.3\%$ at $P{=}32$), but the evidence is single-seed on synthetic data. Validating the frequency-precision gradient on natural video with diffusion architectures (DiT/MMDiT with 3D RoPE) is a natural next step. We also do not elevate short-step LoRA or instruction-tuning evidence, because it confounds positional adaptation with task adaptation.
