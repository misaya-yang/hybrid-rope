\section{Limitations and Scope}

The theory has one explicit approximation: the broadband surrogate. Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport} provides a static-theory derivation of the $L^{-1/2}$ exponent via softmax transport, but the Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ remains a free parameter; $\tau^*$ is therefore a \emph{semi-analytic result} rather than a parameter-free theorem. For video DiT, a modality-dependent correction is needed (${\approx}0.53\times$; Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-correction}).

EVQ requires from-scratch training or very early injection. LoRA (rank~$r < d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$) creates a weight--frequency coupling (${\propto}\tau^2$) that overwhelms the transport utility via a sharp rank phase transition at $r_c{=}K{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$, explaining LLaMA-8B LoRA failure ($r{=}16$, PPL~77.1; Appendix~\ref{sec:lora-regime}). Empirical evidence spans 50M--750M text and 382M video DiT; validation at ${\geq}1$B remains a gap.
