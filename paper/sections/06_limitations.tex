\section{Limitations and Scope}

The theory has one explicit approximation: the broadband surrogate. Accordingly, the surrogate-to-ODE derivation is exact only conditioned on that surrogate, and the scaling law \(\tau^*(L)=d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}\) remains an empirical law / conjecture rather than an unconditional theorem.

Empirically, the primary evidence is still multi-seed text extrapolation. The \(750\mathrm{M}\) continue result is single-seed supporting evidence, and its broader downstream comparison is incomplete because of a dataset-download failure. Cross-modal evidence is supportive rather than co-primary. The autoregressive video results (Tables~\ref{tab:video-temporal}--\ref{tab:freq-decomp}) are single-seed on synthetic data. A separate DiT (diffusion transformer, bidirectional attention) head-to-head experiment confirms EVQ's advantage across two seeds ($-32\%$ far-frame MSE on average; Table~\ref{tab:dit-h2h}), extending validation to non-autoregressive architectures, but remains on synthetic video. Validation on natural video at production scale is an important next step. EVQ outperforms Geo under all tested inference-time methods (YaRN, RIFLEx~\citep{wang2025riflex}, and raw; Table~\ref{tab:dit-inference}), and EVQ's raw performance nearly matches Geo+RIFLEx, suggesting partial overlap between training-time and inference-time frequency correction. A base-reduction experiment further validates the dead-channel mechanism: at base${}=1{,}000$, where the geometric allocation already activates 13/16 temporal channels, the sharp phase transition between $\tau{=}1.2$ and $\tau{=}1.5$ vanishes entirely, with both winning Geo by ${\approx}48\%$ far-frame MSE (Table~\ref{tab:dit-base1000}). Validating this on large pretrained video DiTs is a natural next step. We also do not elevate short-step LoRA or instruction-tuning evidence, because it confounds positional adaptation with task adaptation.
