\section{Limitations and Scope}

\paragraph{Theoretical approximation.} The theory has one explicit approximation: the broadband surrogate $K_{\mathrm{app}} = \alpha\delta + \beta\min$. While this surrogate is functionally validated across 12 configurations (Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation}), pointwise fidelity to the oscillatory exact kernel is not claimed. Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport} derives the $L^{-1/2}$ exponent via softmax transport, but the Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ remains a free parameter calibrated against empirical $\tau^*$ values; the scaling law is therefore a \emph{semi-analytic result} rather than a parameter-free theorem.

\paragraph{Training regime.} EVQ requires from-scratch training or very early injection into continued pretraining. Low-rank adaptation (LoRA with rank $r < d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$) creates a weight--frequency coupling that overwhelms the transport utility via a sharp phase transition at $r_c = d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$, as confirmed by the LLaMA-8B LoRA failure ($r{=}16$, PPL~77.1; Appendix~\ref{sec:lora-regime}). This limits EVQ's applicability to post-training adaptation workflows.

\paragraph{Scale.} Empirical evidence spans 50M--750M text models and 129M--382M video DiT. Validation at ${\geq}1\mathrm{B}$ parameters remains a gap. While the theoretical mechanism (collision reduction) is scale-agnostic, interaction with production-scale training dynamics (learning rate schedules, gradient accumulation, mixture-of-experts) is untested.

\paragraph{Modality dependence.} The $\tau^*$ formula requires a modality-dependent correction for video DiT (${\approx}0.53\times$; Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-correction}), and the decomposition into bidirectional and noise-attenuation factors is post-hoc rather than derived. A principled modality-aware formula for $\tau^*$ remains future work.

\paragraph{Evaluation scope.} The strongest evidence is concentrated on PPL and retrieval-style metrics. Downstream task evaluation (QuALITY QA) is limited by model capacity at the tested scales, and broader long-context benchmarks (LongBench, RULER) are incomplete due to infrastructure constraints at 750M scale. The progressive training result is single-seed.
