\section{Limitations and Scope}

\paragraph{Theoretical approximation.} The broadband surrogate $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ is validated functionally (Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation}) but pointwise fidelity to the oscillatory exact kernel is not claimed. The $\tau^*$ scaling law is semi-analytic: $\lambda$ is calibrated empirically.

\paragraph{Post-training injection.} LoRA injection exhibits a phase transition at $r_c{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:lora-regime}); PPL gains do not transfer to generation-based benchmarks (RULER), and 21-task LongBench on Qwen2.5-7B yields $-0.35\%$ overall (not significant), pointing to a training--evaluation mismatch. Plug-and-play deployment likely requires domain-matched fine-tuning.

\paragraph{Scale and evaluation.} Evidence spans 50M--750M text and 129M--382M video DiT; validation at ${\geq}1\mathrm{B}$ remains a gap. The $\tau^*$ formula requires a modality-dependent correction for video (${\approx}0.53\times$; Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-correction}). Downstream evaluation (QuALITY QA) is limited by model capacity; the progressive result is single-seed.
