\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

Taken together, the results separate three RoPE design choices that are often conflated: the rotary operator, inference-time range scaling, and training-time frequency allocation. EVQ-Cosh leaves the operator and optimizer unchanged, yet changes how YaRN acts, how PE-dominant extrapolation fails, and how compressed-RoPE MLA uses scarce channels.

This is a PE mechanism study, not a universal long-context recipe. Strongest claims use PE diagnostics---extrapolation PPL, teacher-forced NLL-gap retrieval, passkey/NIAH probes, and gold-answer NLL---not universal downstream accuracy. The variational solution is exact for the stated surrogate, but its trained-model link is functionally validated, not derived from full attention dynamics. The rule $\tau=d_{\mathrm{eff}}/\sqrt{L}$ is an empirical-basin default, not a global optimum. MLA convention and production-scale per-channel ablations remain future work; video/LoRA/progressive rows are supporting, not primary.
The likely positive impact is systems-oriented: better spectral allocation may reduce wasted long-context compute and improve reliability in the tested RoPE settings. The main risk is over-generalization: PE diagnostic gains are not deployment safety or reliability guarantees.
