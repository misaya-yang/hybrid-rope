\section{Limitations and Scope}

The theory has one explicit approximation: the broadband surrogate. The surrogate-to-ODE derivation is exact only conditioned on that surrogate, and the scaling law \(\tau^*(L)=d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}\) remains an empirical conjecture. The practical method uses the pure-tether branch only (see Appendix~\ref{sec:proofs} for justification). For video DiT, the scaling law requires a modality-dependent correction (${\approx}0.53\times$; see Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-correction}).

Empirically, the primary evidence is multi-seed text extrapolation at 50M--454M, supplemented by 3-seed MLA validation at 432M ($d_{\mathrm{rope}}{=}32$), with single-seed supporting evidence at 750M (text) and 382M (video DiT). Evidence at ${\geq}1$B with complete downstream evaluation remains a gap. The MLA experiment uses $L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}8192$ with 500M tokens; validation on longer MLA training runs at production scale would strengthen the finding. Cross-modal evidence (autoregressive video and DiT head-to-head) is supportive rather than co-primary: both remain on synthetic video, and validation on natural video at production scale is an important next step.
