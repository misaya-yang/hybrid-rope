\section{Conclusion}

We cast RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form family in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. Across both PE-dominant and larger-scale settings, EVQ improves extreme extrapolation and, more importantly, unlocks inference-time scaling: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN on long-context extrapolation. The practical lesson is that long-context systems depend not only on better inference-time scaling rules, but also on a better training-time frequency substrate.
