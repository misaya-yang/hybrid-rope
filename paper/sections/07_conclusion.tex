\section{Conclusion}

Frequency allocation across RoPE channels has been fixed at the geometric schedule since RoFormer. We show this schedule is the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit of a variational family, and that departing from it---even via a deliberately simple, semi-analytic rule---produces large, consistent gains. The practical temperature $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ is sub-optimal by construction yet matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations, implying the structural correction matters more than the precision of the rule. The strongest systems result is compositional: EVQ provides the training-time frequency substrate that YaRN needs, achieving $100\%$ retrieval where Geo+YaRN plateaus at $61\%$. On MLA with only 16 RoPE channels, EVQ alone outperforms Geo+YaRN, confirming that allocation quality dominates when channels are scarce. As architectures compress positional information into fewer dimensions, principled frequency allocation becomes increasingly consequential---the MLA result, where EVQ alone outperforms Geo+YaRN with only 16 channels, illustrates this directly.
