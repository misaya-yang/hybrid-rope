\section{Conclusion}

We identify frequency allocation as a fundamental design axis for RoPE and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form variational family where geometric RoPE is the $\tau{=}0$ limit. EVQ yields consistent gains: on MHA, EVQ+YaRN extends functional context to $48\mathrm{K}$ ($24\times$ training length); on MLA (16 channels), EVQ alone outperforms Geo+YaRN at $2\times$ extrapolation. The practical lesson is that long-context systems depend crucially on the training-time frequency substrate. As architectures compress RoPE into fewer channels, principled allocation becomes a prerequisite rather than a refinement.
