\section{Conclusion}

We cast RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form family in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. Across PE-dominant and larger-scale settings, EVQ improves extreme extrapolation and unlocks inference-time scaling: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN, extending functional context to \(48\mathrm{K}\) (\(24\times\) training length) at PPL~\(2.63\). Cross-modal transfer to video DiT confirms the frequency-resolved precision gradient ($-32\%$ far-frame MSE). On Multi-head Latent Attention (DeepSeek-V2/V3), EVQ alone outperforms GEO+YaRN at 2--3$\times$ extrapolation (3-seed, $-31.1\%$ PPL at 16K), confirming that allocation optimization is most critical when the frequency budget is small. The practical lesson: long-context systems depend not only on inference-time scaling rules, but also on a better training-time frequency substrate.
