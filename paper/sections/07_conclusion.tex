\section{Conclusion}

We cast RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form family in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. Across both PE-dominant and larger-scale settings, EVQ improves extreme extrapolation and, more importantly, unlocks inference-time scaling: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN on long-context extrapolation. Progressive training widens this advantage monotonically---EVQ+YaRN remains stable while geometric RoPE degrades---extending functional context to \(48\mathrm{K}\) (\(24\times\) training length) at PPL~\(2.63\). Cross-modal transfer experiments on video temporal extrapolation provide further supporting evidence, with frequency-resolved teacher-forced evaluation showing a monotonic precision gradient matching the theoretical prediction; a separate DiT (bidirectional attention) head-to-head experiment confirms these gains across two seeds ($-32\%$ far-frame MSE). The practical lesson is that long-context systems depend not only on better inference-time scaling rules, but also on a better training-time frequency substrate.
