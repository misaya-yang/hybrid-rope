\section{Conclusion}

Channel-frequency allocation is a consequential RoPE design axis that prior work has approached only heuristically. Our variational formulation identifies the geometric schedule as a degenerate boundary case rather than a generic optimum, and a minimal semi-analytic correction---one line of code, zero extra parameters---already yields consistent long-context gains and composes orthogonally with inference-time scaling; the effect is most pronounced where channels are scarce (e.g., MLA), consistent with allocation \emph{shape}---not just \emph{range}---being a binding constraint. We view EVQ-cosh as a starting substrate rather than a final answer; richer families, learned allocations, and modality-conditioned schedules are natural extensions along the axis this paper identifies.
