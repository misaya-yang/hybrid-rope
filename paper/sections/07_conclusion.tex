\section{Conclusion}

This paper establishes that channel-frequency allocation---fixed at the geometric schedule since RoFormer---is a consequential yet underexplored RoPE design axis. The geometric schedule is not a generic optimum but the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit of a variational family; departing from it via a deliberately simple, semi-analytic rule produces large, consistent gains. The practical temperature $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ is sub-optimal by construction yet matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations, implying the structural correction matters more than the precision of the rule. The strongest systems result is compositional: EVQ+YaRN achieves $100\%$ retrieval where Geo+YaRN plateaus at $61\%$. As architectures compress positional information into fewer channels---MLA retains only 16, where EVQ alone outperforms Geo+YaRN---principled frequency allocation becomes particularly decisive. Open directions include closing $\lambda$ analytically and testing basin persistence beyond 7B.
