\section{Conclusion}

We cast RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form family in which geometric RoPE is the degenerate \(\tau=0\) limit. Across both PE-dominant and larger-scale settings, EVQ improves extreme extrapolation and, more importantly, unlocks inference-time scaling: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN on long-context extrapolation. Cross-modal transfer experiments on video temporal extrapolation provide supporting evidence: frequency-resolved teacher-forced evaluation shows a monotonic precision gradient matching the theoretical prediction, with the largest gains at the highest temporal frequencies. The practical lesson is that long-context systems depend not only on better inference-time scaling rules, but also on a better training-time frequency substrate.
