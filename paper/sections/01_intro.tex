\section{Introduction}

Rotary position embedding (RoPE; \citealp{su2024roformer}) has become the dominant positional mechanism in modern transformers, from LLaMA-3 \citep{grattafiori2024llama3} and Qwen2.5 \citep{qwen2024qwen25} to DeepSeek-V3 \citep{deepseekv3} and video DiTs \citep{videorope2025}. Two design axes are routinely tuned: the \emph{base frequency} $b$ is adjusted to match training length, and \emph{inference-time position rescaling}---NTK-aware scaling \citep{roziere2023codellama}, positional interpolation \citep{chen2024position}, YaRN \citep{peng2024yarn}, and LongRoPE \citep{ding2024longrope, shang2025longrope2}---extends the usable range on a frozen checkpoint.

A third axis has received substantially less direct attention: how the $d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$ frequency channels are \emph{allocated} across the spectrum at training time. Since RoFormer, essentially every mainstream implementation has inherited the same geometric (equal-ratio) schedule, treated as a fixed constant rather than a variable to be chosen. Prior work has touched this axis only obliquely. \citet{barbero2025round} observe empirically that high- and low-frequency channels specialize differently after training; \citet{videorope2025} propose modality-specific allocations for video; \citet{wang2024resonance} preserve critical frequencies during interpolation; \citet{shang2025longrope2} search per-channel \emph{post-hoc} rescaling factors via evolution on a frozen checkpoint. What has been missing is a closed-form variational treatment of the \emph{training-time} allocation itself.

We study this axis. Treating a discrete RoPE design as a density $\rho(\phi)$ over log-frequency $\phi\in[0,1]$, we write a variational objective under a broadband functional surrogate of the phase-collision kernel and obtain \textsc{EVQ-cosh}, a closed-form one-parameter family in which geometric RoPE appears as the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate boundary. The resulting warp changes only the inverse-frequency initialization, adds no learned parameters, and is invertible in closed form. Complementing the exact variational layer, a semi-analytic rule $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ selects a robust operating point inside a wide non-geometric basin; we do not claim it is the unique optimum---its role is to move the allocation away from the degenerate $\tau{=}0$ edge rather than to pinpoint a perfect $\tau$.

\paragraph{Empirical scope.} We report three \emph{primary} 3-seed settings: (P1)~EVQ as a training-time substrate for YaRN in a $454$M from-scratch transformer; (P2)~PE-dominant extreme extrapolation ($128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$) against DAPE \citep{zheng2024dape}; (P3)~MLA \citep{deepseekv2, deepseekv3} with $16$ RoPE channels at $432$M, where channels are scarce and EVQ's effect is largest. We additionally report \emph{robustness} experiments on the same MHA setting, and \emph{supporting / exploratory} single- or two-seed evidence (video DiT, LoRA into LLaMA-3-8B, progressive training) whose role is scope-extending rather than claim-bearing. \paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Axis formalization.} We formulate RoPE channel-frequency allocation as a variational problem over densities on log-frequency, and obtain a broadband collision functional whose stationary ODE admits a closed-form family.
\item \textbf{Closed-form EVQ-cosh family.} We derive an invertible one-parameter allocation $\phi_k(\tau)$ in which geometric RoPE appears as the $\tau{=}0$ boundary under the proposed surrogate; the method modifies only the inverse-frequency initialization and adds no learned parameters.
\item \textbf{Robust operating-point rule.} We show that a single semi-analytic rule $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$---with derived $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ and $L^{-1/2}$ structure and an empirically calibrated $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale---selects an allocation inside a wide non-geometric basin, within $<1\%$ PPL of the best observed sweep points in the validated settings.
\item \textbf{Empirical validation with explicit tiering.} We provide 3-seed evidence in MHA, PE-dominant extrapolation, and MLA, together with supporting single- or two-seed evidence on video DiT and LoRA at $7$B; the latter are reported as preliminary.
\end{itemize}
