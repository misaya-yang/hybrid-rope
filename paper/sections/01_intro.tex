\section{Introduction}

Rotary position embedding (RoPE) has three design axes: the base frequency $b$, inference-time position scaling (NTK-aware scaling, YaRN, LongRoPE) \citep{chen2024position, peng2024yarn, ding2024longrope}, and the allocation of frequencies \emph{across channels}. The first two have been extensively studied. The third---how the $d_{\mathrm{head}}/2$ frequency channels should be \emph{distributed} across the spectrum---has received only scattered, heuristic treatment: modality-specific variants \citep{videorope2025}, resonance-based constructions \citep{wang2024resonance}, or empirical observations that trained models utilize frequencies non-uniformly \citep{barbero2025round}. No general principled framework for this allocation exists, and since RoFormer \citep{su2024roformer}, every mainstream implementation has inherited the same fixed geometric (equal-ratio) schedule---treated as a constant, not a variable to be optimized.

This paper shows that the geometric schedule is a degenerate limiting case---the $\tau{=}0$ boundary of a broader variational family---rather than a generic optimum. We formulate frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem, derive a closed-form one-parameter family, EVQ-cosh (Explicit Variational Quantization in cosh form), and demonstrate that even a deliberately simple, semi-analytic temperature rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ already produces large, consistent gains across architectures and scales with zero additional parameters. The core mechanism is intuitive: geometric RoPE distributes frequency mass uniformly in log-space, wasting channels on redundant high-frequency distinctions while starving the low-frequency regime that governs long-range discrimination. EVQ redistributes this mass via a variational objective that trades local resolution against long-range coverage.

\paragraph{Why this matters now.} The frequency allocation axis becomes particularly consequential as architectures compress RoPE into fewer channels. In standard MHA with 64 channels, geometric allocation wastes capacity quietly. In MLA-style designs \citep{deepseekv2, deepseekv3} with only 16 RoPE channels, every channel's placement is decisive: EVQ alone reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL by $31.1\%$ relative to Geo baseline, surpassing even Geo+YaRN (3~seeds), demonstrating that allocation quality dominates when channels are scarce.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\textbf{(i)~Axis identification and variational framework.} We identify channel-frequency allocation as a RoPE design axis previously characterized only through heuristic or learned approaches, and derive EVQ-cosh: a closed-form variational family in which geometric RoPE is the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit. The method modifies only the inverse-frequency initialization, adds zero learned parameters, and leaves the transformer architecture unchanged.
\textbf{(ii)~A semi-analytic rule matches per-setting optima.} A single rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$---whose $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ and $L^{-1/2}$ structure is derived and whose remaining $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale is empirically validated---matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations (12 primary 3-seed + 15 robustness/supporting) spanning MHA (50M--750M), MLA (16 channels), video DiT, and post-hoc LoRA at 7B, with training lengths from $L{=}32$ to $L{=}8192$. The value of EVQ lies in the \emph{direction} of correction---away from $\tau{=}0$---not in the precision of the rule.
\textbf{(iii)~Complementary composition with inference-time scaling.} Training-time allocation and inference-time rescaling address orthogonal deficiencies---EVQ corrects \emph{shape} (non-uniform density), YaRN corrects \emph{range}---so they compose additively. EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ passkey retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ where Geo+YaRN remains at $61\%$ (3~seeds), with $14$--$32\%$ PPL reductions throughout $8$--$16\mathrm{K}$; NTK-aware scaling, by contrast, partially overwrites the optimized frequencies and is destructive. In the PE-dominant regime ($128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$), EVQ alone---with zero extra parameters---beats DAPE \citep{zheng2024dape}, a 32-parameter learned positional encoding ($333.7$ vs.\ $455.3$ PPL), isolating frequency structure itself as the binding variable.
