\section{Introduction}

The quality of rotary position embedding (RoPE) is governed by three design axes: the base frequency $b$, the head dimension $d_{\mathrm{head}}$, and the allocation of frequencies across channels. The first axis has received sustained attention---NTK-aware scaling, YaRN, and related methods all operate by adjusting $b$ or its effective value at inference time \citep{chen2023position, peng2024yarn, ding2024longrope}. The second axis is typically set by architecture constraints and has become newly relevant as compressed designs such as Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA) reduce the number of RoPE channels from 64 to as few as 16. The third axis---how frequency mass is distributed among channels---has remained essentially unchanged since RoFormer \citep{su2024roformer}: every mainstream implementation uses a geometric (equal-ratio) schedule.

This paper shows that geometric allocation is not a generic optimum but rather a degenerate limiting case, and that replacing it with a principled allocation yields consistent improvements across architectures. We formulate RoPE frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem, derive a broadband surrogate in which the stationary density satisfies a second-order ODE, and obtain a closed-form family, EVQ-cosh, parameterized by a single temperature $\tau$. In this family, geometric RoPE appears at $\tau=0$. The solution modifies only the inverse-frequency initialization, adds zero learned parameters, and leaves the transformer architecture unchanged---making it a drop-in replacement compatible with any RoPE-based model.

This change in the frequency substrate leads to three empirical findings spanning different architectures and regimes:

\paragraph{MHA: EVQ unlocks inference-time scaling.} On standard multi-head attention (50M--750M), EVQ provides a substantially better training-time substrate for inference-time methods. Across three seeds per configuration (six total), EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ while Geo+YaRN remains at $61\%$, with PPL improvements of $14\text{--}32\%$ at $8\text{--}16\mathrm{K}$. Progressive context extension (single seed) widens this gap monotonically, extending functional context to $48\mathrm{K}$ ($24\times$ training length) at PPL~$2.63$.

\paragraph{MLA: principled allocation matters more when channels are scarce.} On MLA (432M, 3-seed), where only 16 frequency channels carry positional information, EVQ alone reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL by $31.1\%$ and outperforms Geo+YaRN at $16\mathrm{K}$. When every channel's placement counts, the gap between geometric and optimal allocation is amplified.

\paragraph{PE-dominant regime: frequency structure alone is decisive.} In DAPE-style extreme extrapolation ($128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$), EVQ surpasses learnable adaptive positional encoding with zero additional parameters ($333.7$ vs.\ $455.3$ PPL), isolating frequency allocation itself as the decisive variable.

The paper's claim is therefore not merely that EVQ improves long-context performance, but that frequency allocation is a fundamental and under-explored design axis for rotary position embedding. Geometric RoPE has two orthogonal deficiencies: a \emph{shape} deficiency (uniform log-frequency density wastes channels) and a \emph{range} deficiency (frequencies designed for $L_{\mathrm{train}}$ cannot cover longer distances). EVQ addresses shape; inference-time methods such as YaRN address range. Because the corrections operate on different axes, their benefits compose rather than compete.

Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
\item We identify frequency allocation as a fundamental PE design axis and derive EVQ-cosh, a closed-form variational solution in which geometric RoPE is the $\tau=0$ degenerate case of a broader optimal family.
\item We demonstrate architecture-agnostic gains: EVQ improves MHA (50M--750M text), MLA (432M, 16 RoPE channels), and video DiT, with a single formula $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ selecting the allocation across all settings.
\item We show that the strongest systems result is the composition: EVQ unlocks YaRN, with training-time and inference-time positional optimization being orthogonal and strongly complementary.
\item We show that progressive training widens EVQ's advantage monotonically (single seed): three-stage context extension ($512{\to}1024{\to}2048$) yields a growing relative PPL advantage ($-34.6\%{\to}{-52.0\%}{\to}{-81.2\%}$ at $16\mathrm{K}$), extending functional context to $48\mathrm{K}$ ($24\times$ training length) at PPL~$2.63$.
\end{itemize}
