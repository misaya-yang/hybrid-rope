\section{Introduction}

Rotary position embedding (RoPE) has two well-studied design axes: the base frequency $b$ and the head dimension $d_{\mathrm{head}}$. Inference-time methods---NTK-aware scaling, YaRN, LongRoPE---all operate on the first axis \citep{chen2024position, peng2024yarn, ding2024longrope}; the second is set by architecture constraints. A third axis exists but has received almost no attention: the allocation of frequencies \emph{across channels}. Since RoFormer \citep{su2024roformer}, every mainstream implementation has used a geometric (equal-ratio) schedule for this allocation. The schedule is treated as a fixed design choice, not a variable.

This paper shows that the geometric schedule is a degenerate limiting case---the $\tau{=}0$ boundary of a broader variational family---rather than a generic optimum. We formulate frequency allocation as a variational inverse problem, derive a closed-form one-parameter family (EVQ-cosh), and demonstrate that even a deliberately simple, semi-analytic temperature rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ already produces large, consistent gains across architectures and scales with zero additional parameters. The core mechanism is intuitive: geometric RoPE distributes frequency mass uniformly in log-space, wasting channels on redundant high-frequency distinctions while starving the low-frequency regime that governs long-range discrimination. EVQ redistributes this mass toward an information-theoretically motivated allocation.

\paragraph{Why this matters now.} The frequency allocation axis becomes increasingly consequential as architectures compress RoPE into fewer channels. In standard MHA with 64 channels, geometric allocation wastes capacity quietly. In MLA-style designs (DeepSeek-V2/V3) with only 16 RoPE channels, every channel's placement is decisive: EVQ alone outperforms Geo+YaRN at $2\times$ extrapolation ($-31.1\%$ PPL, 3~seeds), demonstrating that allocation quality dominates when channels are scarce.

Three empirical findings anchor the paper:

\paragraph{EVQ unlocks inference-time scaling (primary systems result).} Training-time frequency allocation and inference-time rescaling address orthogonal deficiencies: EVQ corrects \emph{shape} (non-uniform density), YaRN corrects \emph{range}. Their composition is strongly complementary. On MHA (3~seeds per configuration), EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ passkey retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ where Geo+YaRN remains at $61\%$, with PPL improvements of $14$--$32\%$ at $8$--$16\mathrm{K}$.

\paragraph{Allocation alone is decisive in the PE-dominant regime.} In DAPE-style extreme extrapolation ($128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$), EVQ surpasses learnable positional encoding with zero additional parameters ($333.7$ vs.\ $455.3$ PPL), isolating frequency structure itself as the binding variable.

\paragraph{A sub-optimal rule suffices.} The practical $\tau^*$ formula is semi-analytic: $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ dependence and the $L^{-1/2}$ exponent are derived, but one transport constant is calibrated empirically. Despite this deliberate sub-optimality, the rule matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across 27 configurations (99 runs), indicating a wide robustness basin. The structural correction---redistributing frequency mass---does the work; the precision of the rule that selects it is secondary.

\medskip
\noindent Our contributions:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Axis identification and variational framework.} We identify channel-frequency allocation as a previously neglected RoPE design axis, and derive EVQ-cosh: a closed-form variational family in which geometric RoPE is the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit. The method modifies only the inverse-frequency initialization, adds zero parameters, and leaves the transformer architecture unchanged.

\item \textbf{Near-oracle sub-optimality.} A single semi-analytic rule $\tau^* = d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$, deliberately sub-optimal by construction, matches per-setting optima to within $<1\%$ PPL across MHA (50M--750M), MLA (16 channels), video DiT, and post-hoc LoRA at 7B---27 configurations spanning three orders of magnitude in training length. This wide robustness basin is itself a structural finding about the frequency allocation landscape.

\item \textbf{Complementary composition with inference-time scaling.} EVQ provides a better training-time frequency substrate for inference-time methods. EVQ+YaRN produces the paper's strongest systems result: $100\%$ retrieval (vs.\ $61\%$ for Geo+YaRN at $8\mathrm{K}$, 3~seeds per configuration) with $14$--$32\%$ PPL reductions, confirming that training-time and inference-time positional optimization address orthogonal deficiencies rather than competing.
\end{itemize}
