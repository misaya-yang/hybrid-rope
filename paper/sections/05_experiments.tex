\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Setup}

All core experiments are from-scratch and keep the model architecture fixed. We use \(b=500\mathrm{K}\), the predicted \(\tau^*(L)\) for each training length, and modify only the RoPE inverse-frequency initialization. The primary datasets are TinyStories, FineWeb-Edu, and controlled passkey-mix settings; the main long-context evaluations are retrieval-style extrapolation and broader suites such as LongBench and RULER when available \citep{bai2024longbench, hsieh2024ruler}. Architecturally, the models follow standard GPT-style decoder stacks \citep{radford2019gpt2} with $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}64$ across all scales (50M--750M), and no extra positional network is trained.

Primary body claims are anchored in multi-seed evidence. The core raw-PPL and passkey-mix comparisons use three seeds, and the strongest EVQ\(\times\)YaRN retrieval result aggregates six seeds across the relevant fair-comparison settings. Evaluation is split deliberately: retrieval-style tasks test whether information can still be localized at long range, while stricter AR-exact and multi-needle variants test whether that information remains recoverable with high precision. Full hyperparameters, dataset construction details, and appendix-only baselines are reserved for the supplementary material.

\subsection{EVQ unlocks YaRN}

Figure~\ref{fig:evq-yarn} and Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn} contain the paper's main systems result. The decisive comparison is not EVQ against geometric RoPE in isolation, but EVQ combined with YaRN against Geo+YaRN. In the fair \(10\%\) mix comparison, EVQ+YaRN reaches \(100\%\) retrieval at \(8\mathrm{K}\) across the three seeds used in that setting, while Geo+YaRN remains at \(61\%\); the same combination lowers PPL from \(82.9\) to \(70.9\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) and from \(157.7\) to \(107.5\) at \(16\mathrm{K}\).

Two aspects of Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn} matter. First, EVQ alone is helpful but not dominant: the raw EVQ row improves over Geo without saturating retrieval, so the sharp jump to \(100\%\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) under YaRN cannot be dismissed as a ceiling effect. Second, the corresponding PPL reductions confirm that the retrieval gain is not a narrow metric artifact.

\subsection{Extreme extrapolation and scaling-law confirmation}

Figure~\ref{fig:pe-dominant} provides the second principal empirical anchor. In the DAPE-style PE-dominant regime, EVQ beats learnable PE with zero extra parameters: on the \(128\!\rightarrow\!8\mathrm{K}\) setup, EVQ reaches \(333.7\) PPL while DAPE reaches \(455.3\), isolating positional encoding itself as the decisive variable and showing that a learned positional module can still miss the closed-form allocation favored by the variational theory.

The same figure carries the \(L=256\) Phase 11 confirmation. The theory predicts \(\tau^*=4.0\), and the raw plus YaRN curves show exactly the expected ordering: the raw \(32\times\) curve drops from \(268.4\) to \(167.8\), and EVQ4+YaRN reduces PPL from \(260.2\) to \(99.6\). Critically, the same \(\tau^*=4.0\) is preferred across \(125\mathrm{M}\) and \(454\mathrm{M}\) --- model scale changes extrapolation difficulty but not which EVQ setting is optimal.

\subsection{Robustness and capability preservation}

Table~\ref{tab:multiscale} summarizes the raw multi-scale pattern. Across model scales, EVQ pays at most a small in-range cost while improving long-range PPL. Table~\ref{tab:capability} complements this with capability-oriented evidence: when retrieval is absent from training, EVQ is non-destructive; once retrieval is learned, EVQ improves extrapolated retrieval and is markedly more robust to denser passkey supervision.

\input{tables/table3_capability_passkey}

The experiments support two narrower properties rather than a universal improvement claim: when a task is absent from training, EVQ is approximately non-destructive; when a retrieval-style task is learned, EVQ improves its extrapolated performance beyond \(L_{\mathrm{train}}\). Downstream evaluation on QuALITY QA (\(n{=}2086\), 454M finetuned) confirms this: Gold Answer NLL improves by \(-30\%\) at \(2\times\) extrapolation (\(8\mathrm{K}\)), with accuracy \(+2.2\mathrm{pp}\) (\(p{\approx}0.02\)); full details are in the appendix.

A larger-scale consistency check at \(750\mathrm{M}\) (continue@4K, single-seed; see Appendix~\ref{sec:750m-supporting}) preserves the same qualitative pattern: PPL improves by \(16.2\%\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) and \(45.9\%\) at \(16\mathrm{K}\), while \(8\mathrm{K}\) AR exact rises from \(0\%\) to \(77.5\%\).

\paragraph{Progressive training widens EVQ's advantage.}
Three-stage progressive training (454M, \(512\!\rightarrow\!1024\!\rightarrow\!2048\)) reveals that EVQ's advantage grows monotonically across stages. Table~\ref{tab:progressive} shows the relative EVQ+YaRN PPL advantage at \(16\mathrm{K}\) widening from \(-34.6\%\) (Stage~1) to \(-52.0\%\) (Stage~2) to \(-81.2\%\) (Stage~3). Notably, EVQ+YaRN remains stable across stages (PPL $2.48 \to 2.21 \to 2.48$), while Geo+YaRN degrades sharply ($3.80 \to 4.60 \to 13.17$). The widening gap is thus primarily driven by geometric RoPE's progressive failure at longer training lengths, not by intrinsic EVQ improvement --- but this is itself strong evidence that EVQ's frequency substrate is fundamentally more robust to context extension. At Stage~3, EVQ+YaRN extends functional context to \(48\mathrm{K}\) (\(24\times\) training length) at PPL~\(2.63\), an \(82\%\) improvement over Geo+YaRN's \(14.22\). Passkey retrieval reaches \(100\%\) at all tested lengths (\(2\mathrm{K}\)--\(16\mathrm{K}\)).
