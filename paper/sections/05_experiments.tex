\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Setup and evidence tiering}
\label{sec:exp-setup}

\paragraph{Evaluation scope.} This is a positional-encoding mechanism study. PE-diagnostic endpoints (extrapolation PPL, passkey, NIAH/RULER-style retrieval, gold-answer NLL) are our primary mechanistic endpoints; PK denotes teacher-forced NLL-gap retrieval unless explicitly marked as autoregressive exact match. Downstream accuracy at these scales is a non-regression check rather than a primary claim. EVQ-Cosh modifies only the training-time allocation and adds no learned parameters---it is neither an inference-time rescaler (YaRN/NTK/LongRoPE) nor a learned positional operator (DAPE/FIRE).

Experiments test three questions: (Q1)~does EVQ improve over Geo at fixed training length? (Q2)~does EVQ compose with inference-time scaling under matched YaRN scale? (Q3)~does the $\tau^*$ rule land in the best observed sweep basin? Core runs train from scratch with fixed architecture, changing only the RoPE inverse-frequency initialization. We use $b{=}500\mathrm{K}$, predicted $\tau^*(L)$, and GPT-style decoder stacks \citep{radford2019gpt2} with $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}64$ across $50$M--$750$M. Primary text data are FineWeb-Edu and controlled passkey-mix settings; TinyStories appears only in supporting quick-validation runs. Full hyperparameters are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supporting-experiments}.

Evidence is tiered in Table~\ref{tab:evidence-tier}. Primary claims use EVQ$\times$YaRN (3-seed), PE-dominant extrapolation (seed-42 diagnostic for Geo/DAPE/EVQ; learned-PE row 3-seed), and MLA (3-seed). Robustness rows are 3-seed; supporting/exploratory rows are 1--2 seed. No abstract or introduction claim rests on tier-3 evidence.

\subsection{Primary I: EVQ$\times$YaRN matched-scale substrate test}
\label{sec:exp-yarn}

\emph{Substrate vs.\ range stress test.} If EVQ only changed effective range, the same YaRN scale should largely erase its advantage; if it changes the training-time substrate, YaRN should have higher leverage on EVQ than Geo (Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn}, Fig.~\ref{fig:evq-yarn}). In the $454$M $10\%$ passkey-mix setting ($3$ seeds), EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ teacher-forced NLL-gap passkey retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ while Geo+YaRN remains at $61\%$, with the advantage persisting at $12$/$16\mathrm{K}$ ($79\%/68\%$ vs.\ $59\%/51\%$); PPL drops from $82.9$ to $70.9$ at $8\mathrm{K}$ and from $157.7$ to $107.5$ at $16\mathrm{K}$. Appendix Table~\ref{tab:phase11-leverage} quantifies the interaction; we read this as increased matched-scale YaRN leverage, not dominance over every tuned-scale YaRN baseline.

\subsection{Primary II: PE-dominant allocation stress test}
\label{sec:exp-pe}

\emph{Allocation-shape stress test.} In a DAPE-style $128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$ protocol \citep{zheng2024dape}, we ask whether allocation shape alone can reduce a PE-dominant extrapolation gap without adding a learned positional operator. Table~\ref{tab:pe-dominant}: in the retained seed-42 Geo/DAPE/EVQ contrast, EVQ reaches $333.7$ PPL (vs.\ Geo $513.7$, DAPE $455.3$) with no learned parameters. We read this as competitiveness in this tested protocol, not comprehensive dominance over DAPE. Complementary \(L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}256\) sweeps support the predicted $\tau^{*}{=}4.0$ setting; Appendix Fig.~\ref{fig:pe-dominant} and Table~\ref{tab:pe-yarn-l256} show the retained Phase~11 curves.

\subsection{Primary III: scarce-channel stress test (MLA)}
\label{sec:exp-mla}

\emph{Scarce-channel stress test.} MLA \citep{deepseekv2, deepseekv3} compresses RoPE to $d_{\mathrm{rope}}{=}32$ ($16$ channels) vs.\ $64$ in MHA at $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}128$, where the finite-budget view predicts stronger EVQ effects. For MLA, $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ determines quantized rotary channels, while $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$ is the transport-scale convention. On a $432$M MLA transformer trained for $500$M tokens at $L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}8192$ ($3$ seeds), EVQ reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL from $138.8$ to $95.6$ ($-31.1\%$) with $+1.1\%$ in-distribution cost, exceeding Geo+YaRN at matched scale $s{=}4$ ($117.9$; Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). This is larger than the matched MHA improvement ($-13.3\%$; Table~\ref{tab:multiscale}), consistent with scarce channels amplifying allocation effects.

\subsection{Robustness and supporting evidence}
\label{sec:exp-robust}
Multi-scale raw extrapolation (Table~\ref{tab:multiscale}) keeps in-range PPL within ${\pm}1.7\%$ while long-range PPL improves $10$--$46\%$; capability checks (Table~\ref{tab:capability}) confirm non-destructiveness. Supporting (1--2 seed) evidence covers video DiT, LoRA into LLaMA-3-8B, progressive training, and a single-seed MLA saturation check; full breakdowns are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supporting-experiments}.
