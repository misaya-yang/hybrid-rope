\section{Experiments}

\subsection{Setup}

All core experiments are from-scratch and keep the model architecture fixed. We use \(b=500\mathrm{K}\), the predicted \(\tau^*(L)\) for each training length, and modify only the RoPE inverse-frequency initialization. The primary datasets are TinyStories, FineWeb-Edu, and controlled passkey-mix settings; the main long-context evaluations are retrieval-style extrapolation and broader suites such as LongBench and RULER when available \citep{bai2024longbench, hsieh2024ruler}. Architecturally, the models follow standard GPT-style decoder stacks \citep{radford2019gpt2} with $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}64$ across all scales (50M--750M), and no extra positional network is trained.

Primary body claims are anchored in multi-seed evidence. The core raw-PPL and passkey-mix comparisons use three seeds, and the strongest EVQ\(\times\)YaRN retrieval result aggregates six seeds across the relevant fair-comparison settings. Evaluation is split deliberately: retrieval-style tasks test whether information can still be localized at long range, while stricter AR-exact and multi-needle variants test whether that information remains recoverable with high precision. Full hyperparameters, dataset construction details, and appendix-only baselines are reserved for the supplementary material.

\subsection{EVQ unlocks YaRN}

Figure~\ref{fig:evq-yarn} and Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn} contain the paper's main systems result. The decisive comparison is not EVQ against geometric RoPE in isolation, but EVQ combined with YaRN against Geo+YaRN. In the fair \(10\%\) mix comparison, EVQ+YaRN reaches \(100\%\) retrieval at \(8\mathrm{K}\) across the three seeds used in that setting, while Geo+YaRN remains at \(61\%\); the same combination lowers PPL from \(82.9\) to \(70.9\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) and from \(157.7\) to \(107.5\) at \(16\mathrm{K}\).

Two aspects of Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn} are worth noting. First, EVQ alone improves over Geo without saturating retrieval, so the sharp jump to \(100\%\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\) under YaRN cannot be dismissed as a ceiling effect. Second, the corresponding PPL reductions confirm that the retrieval gain reflects genuine long-range quality improvement, not a narrow metric artifact.

The composition is not accidental: geometric RoPE has two orthogonal deficiencies that limit long-context performance. A \emph{shape} deficiency---uniform log-frequency density wastes channels on redundant high-frequency coverage---and a \emph{range} deficiency---frequencies designed for \(L_{\mathrm{train}}\) cannot discriminate positions beyond training length. EVQ addresses shape by redistributing density toward the collision-critical low-frequency band; YaRN addresses range by rescaling positions at inference time. Because the corrections operate on different axes of the frequency map, their benefits compose rather than compete.

\subsection{Extreme extrapolation and scaling-law confirmation}

Figure~\ref{fig:pe-dominant} provides the second principal empirical anchor. In the DAPE-style PE-dominant regime, EVQ beats learnable PE with zero extra parameters: on the \(128\!\rightarrow\!8\mathrm{K}\) setup, EVQ reaches \(333.7\) PPL while DAPE reaches \(455.3\), isolating positional encoding itself as the decisive variable and showing that a learned positional module can still miss the closed-form allocation favored by the variational theory.

The same figure carries the \(L=256\) Phase 11 confirmation. The theory predicts \(\tau^*=4.0\), and the raw plus YaRN curves show exactly the expected ordering: the raw \(32\times\) curve drops from \(268.4\) to \(167.8\), and EVQ4+YaRN reduces PPL from \(260.2\) to \(99.6\). Critically, the same \(\tau^*=4.0\) is preferred across \(125\mathrm{M}\) and \(454\mathrm{M}\) --- model scale changes extrapolation difficulty but not which EVQ setting is optimal.

\subsection{Robustness and capability preservation}

Table~\ref{tab:multiscale} summarizes the multi-scale pattern: EVQ pays at most a small in-range cost (\({\leq}1\%\)) while improving long-range PPL by \(10\text{--}46\%\) across 50M--750M scales. Capability-oriented evaluation (350M, 3-seed) confirms two properties: EVQ is non-destructive when retrieval is absent from training, and improves extrapolated retrieval by \(+10\text{--}13\mathrm{pp}\) when retrieval is learned. The base generalization sweep (454M, single seed) confirms EVQ leads at both \(b{=}10\mathrm{K}\) and \(b{=}500\mathrm{K}\), with EVQ's extrapolation PPL nearly identical across base values (\(192.4 \approx 191.9\)) while Geo diverges by 38~points---suggesting that frequency optimization partially compensates for suboptimal base choices.

Downstream QuALITY QA (\(n{=}2086\), 454M, single seed) reveals a consistent signal gradient across evaluation granularity: EVQ's advantage is largest in PPL (\(-30\%\) at \(8\mathrm{K}\)), preserved in Gold-Answer NLL (\(-30\%\)), detectable in passkey retrieval (\(+13\mathrm{pp}\)), and marginally visible in 4-option accuracy (\(+2.2\mathrm{pp}\), \(p{\approx}0.02\))---a progressive abstraction pattern consistent with model capacity being the binding constraint on accuracy, not positional encoding. We treat this as supporting evidence that the PPL/retrieval gains reflect genuine quality improvement, rather than a decisive downstream benchmark. A \(750\mathrm{M}\) continue@4K consistency check (single-seed; Appendix~\ref{sec:750m-supporting}) preserves the same pattern.

\paragraph{Progressive training widens EVQ's advantage.}
Three-stage progressive training (454M, \(512{\to}1024{\to}2048\), single seed) reveals that EVQ's advantage grows monotonically (Table~\ref{tab:progressive}): the EVQ+YaRN PPL advantage at \(16\mathrm{K}\) widens from \(-34.6\%\) to \(-81.2\%\), driven by Geo+YaRN degradation ($3.80 \to 13.17$) while EVQ+YaRN remains stable ($2.48 \to 2.21 \to 2.48$). At Stage~3, EVQ+YaRN extends functional context to \(48\mathrm{K}\) (\(24\times\) training length) at PPL~\(2.63\).

\paragraph{Multi-head Latent Attention.}
EVQ extends to MLA (the attention mechanism in DeepSeek-V2/V3), where only 16 frequency channels carry positional information. On a 432M model (3-seed), EVQ alone reduces \(2\times\) extrapolation PPL by \(31.1\%\) and outperforms GEO+YaRN at \(16\text{--}24\mathrm{K}\); EVQ+YaRN establishes strict dominance at every extrapolation length. Full results are in Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}.
