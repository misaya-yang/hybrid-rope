\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Setup and evidence tiering}
\label{sec:exp-setup}

\paragraph{Evaluation scope.} This is a positional-encoding mechanism study. PE-diagnostic endpoints (extrapolation PPL, passkey, NIAH/RULER-style retrieval, gold-answer NLL) are our primary mechanistic endpoints; PK denotes teacher-forced NLL-gap retrieval unless explicitly marked as autoregressive exact match. Downstream accuracy at these scales is a non-regression check rather than a primary claim. EVQ-Cosh modifies only the training-time allocation and adds no learned parameters---it is neither an inference-time rescaler (YaRN/NTK/LongRoPE) nor a learned positional operator (DAPE/FIRE).

Experiments test three questions derived from the variational characterization: (Q1)~does EVQ improve over Geo at fixed training length? (Q2)~does EVQ compose with inference-time scaling, i.e., does EVQ+YaRN outperform Geo+YaRN at extrapolated lengths under matched YaRN scale? (Q3)~does the $\tau^*$ rule land in the best observed sweep basin across architectures and scales? Core experiments train from scratch with fixed architecture, modifying only the RoPE inverse-frequency initialization. We use $b{=}500\mathrm{K}$, the predicted $\tau^*(L)$ for each training length, and standard GPT-style decoder stacks \citep{radford2019gpt2} with $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}64$ across $50$M--$750$M. The primary datasets are TinyStories, FineWeb-Edu, and controlled passkey-mix settings. Full hyperparameters are in Appendix~\ref{sec:supporting-experiments}.

Evidence is organized in three tiers (Table~\ref{tab:evidence-tier}). \emph{Primary} (directly tied to central claims): EVQ$\times$YaRN composition (\S\ref{sec:exp-yarn}, 3-seed), PE-dominant extrapolation (\S\ref{sec:exp-pe}, seed-42 diagnostic for Geo/DAPE/EVQ; only the learned-PE comparison row is 3-seed), and MLA (\S\ref{sec:exp-mla}, 3-seed). \emph{Robustness} (3-seed): FineWeb-Edu consistency, base sweep, and capability checks (\S\ref{sec:exp-robust}). \emph{Supporting / exploratory} (1--2 seed): one-seed multi-scale rows, video DiT, LoRA into LLaMA-3-8B, progressive training, and MLA 1B-token (Appendix~\ref{sec:supporting-experiments}). No abstract or introduction claim rests on tier-3 evidence.

\subsection{Primary I: EVQ$\times$YaRN matched-scale substrate test}
\label{sec:exp-yarn}

\emph{Substrate vs.\ range stress test.} If EVQ only changed effective range, applying the same YaRN scale should largely erase its advantage; if it changes the training-time substrate, the same rescaling rule should have higher leverage on EVQ than Geo (Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn}, Fig.~\ref{fig:evq-yarn}). In the $454$M $10\%$ passkey-mix setting ($3$~seeds per config), EVQ+YaRN reaches $100\%$ passkey retrieval at $8\mathrm{K}$ while Geo+YaRN remains at $61\%$, with the advantage persisting at $12$/$16\mathrm{K}$ ($79\%/68\%$ vs.\ $59\%/51\%$; Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn}); PPL drops from $82.9$ to $70.9$ at $8\mathrm{K}$ and from $157.7$ to $107.5$ at $16\mathrm{K}$. EVQ alone improves over Geo without saturating retrieval. Appendix Table~\ref{tab:phase11-leverage} quantifies the interaction: YaRN gives Geo only $-4.5\%/-3.1\%$ at $4$/$8\mathrm{K}$ but gives EVQ ($\tau{=}4$) $-32.5\%/-40.7\%$; naive NTK-aware scaling becomes destructive at large $\tau$. We read this as EVQ increasing YaRN's matched-scale leverage, not dominance over every tuned-scale YaRN baseline.

\subsection{Primary II: PE-dominant allocation stress test}
\label{sec:exp-pe}

\emph{Allocation-shape stress test.} In a DAPE-style $128{\to}8\mathrm{K}$ protocol \citep{zheng2024dape} where model capacity is deliberately limited, we ask whether allocation shape alone can reduce a PE-dominant extrapolation gap without adding a learned positional operator. Table~\ref{tab:pe-dominant} (\(L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}128, d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}64\)): in the retained seed-42 DAPE-style Geo/DAPE/EVQ contrast, EVQ reaches $333.7$ PPL (vs.\ Geo $513.7$, DAPE $455.3$) with no learned parameters. We read this as evidence that closed-form reallocation can be competitive with learned positional operators in this tested protocol, not as a comprehensive dominance claim over DAPE. A complementary $\tau$-sweep at \(L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}256\) (Appendix Fig.~\ref{fig:pe-dominant}, numbers in Appendix Table~\ref{tab:pe-yarn-l256}) supports that the formula's prediction $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}{=}4.0$ is favored among tested settings at both $125$M and $454$M.

\subsection{Primary III: scarce-channel stress test (MLA)}
\label{sec:exp-mla}

\emph{Scarce-channel stress test.} MLA \citep{deepseekv2, deepseekv3} compresses RoPE to $d_{\mathrm{rope}}{=}32$ ($16$ channels after pairing) vs.\ $64$ in MHA at $d_{\mathrm{head}}{=}128$; the finite-budget view predicts EVQ's relative effect should \emph{grow} here. For MLA, $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ determines the quantized rotary channels, while $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$ is used as the transport-scale convention in the $\tau$ rule; this convention is empirically validated here but remains architecture-specific rather than a theorem. On a $432$M MLA transformer trained for $500$M tokens at $L_{\mathrm{train}}{=}8192$ ($3$~seeds), EVQ reduces $2\times$ extrapolation PPL from $138.8$ to $95.6$ ($-31.1\%$) with $+1.1\%$ in-distribution cost, exceeding Geo+YaRN at matched YaRN scale $s{=}4$ ($117.9$; Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). The $31.1\%$ MLA effect is larger than the $-13.3\%$ MHA improvement at the matched 3-seed FineWeb-Edu setting (Table~\ref{tab:multiscale}, $16$K column), consistent with the hypothesis that scarce channels amplify the allocation correction.

\subsection{Robustness and supporting evidence.}
\label{sec:exp-robust}
Multi-scale raw extrapolation (Table~\ref{tab:multiscale}) keeps in-range PPL within ${\pm}1.7\%$ while long-range PPL improves $10$--$46\%$; capability checks (Table~\ref{tab:capability}) confirm non-destructiveness. Supporting (1--2 seed): video DiT ($0.53\times$ post-hoc), LoRA into LLaMA-3-8B ($8$--$19\times$ extrapolation-PPL), progressive training, and a single-seed MLA training-saturation check where the YaRN composition boost grows from $+8.6$~pp at 500M to $+13.6$~pp at 1B despite raw EVQ reversal. Effect attenuates through abstraction layers (raw PPL/NLL/passkey/QA; full breakdown Appendix~\ref{sec:supporting-experiments}), concentrated at the PE layer.
