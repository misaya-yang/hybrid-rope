\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Positional encoding design space.}
Transformers rely on positional structure to recover order information \citep{vaswani2017attention}. The design space includes relative attention biases \citep{shaw2018self, raffel2020exploring}, ALiBi \citep{press2022alibi}, and rotary position embedding (RoPE) \citep{su2024roformer}. Within RoPE, most prior work focuses on the base frequency axis (NTK-aware scaling, YaRN) or inference-time rescaling. Our work targets a different axis: the allocation of frequencies across channels, which has remained at the geometric default since RoFormer.

\paragraph{Inference-time context extension.}
Recent long-context methods preserve the training checkpoint and modify positional behavior only at inference time \citep{chen2023position, peng2024yarn, ding2024longrope, anil2022pose, jin2024selfextend}. LongRoPE2~\citep{zhu2024longrope2} searches for per-channel scaling factors via evolution, optimizing inference-time rescaling on a frozen checkpoint. CLEX~\citep{chen2024clex} trains a continuous length-extrapolation module. While both LongRoPE2 and EVQ operate on per-channel frequency structure, they target orthogonal design axes: EVQ modifies the frequency layout \emph{before training}, whereas LongRoPE2 searches for rescaling \emph{after training}. This complementarity is demonstrated empirically: EVQ+YaRN substantially outperforms Geo+YaRN (Table~\ref{tab:evq-yarn}), confirming that a better training-time substrate amplifies inference-time methods rather than competing with them.

\paragraph{Learnable and adaptive positional encoding.}
Another line of work introduces learnable or adaptive positional mechanisms, including Kerple, FIRE, and DAPE \citep{chi2022kerple, li2024fire, zheng2024dape}. These methods are the closest comparison point for our PE-dominant experiments because they ask whether adaptive positional structure can improve extrapolation. Our distinction is that EVQ is derived in closed form rather than learned through additional positional parameters, which lets us separate the question of \emph{what frequency allocation is optimal} from the question of \emph{whether an optimizer can discover it from in-distribution loss alone}.

\paragraph{Video positional encoding.}
Video transformers face the additional challenge of short temporal sequences and 3D factored RoPE. VideoRoPE~\citep{videorope2025} identifies sequence-length-dependent frequency allocation as a key design axis for video generation; FreeNoise~\citep{qin2024freenoise} addresses temporal coherence via noise rescheduling; RIFLEx~\citep{wang2025riflex} corrects intrinsic frequency at inference time. EVQ is complementary: it optimizes the training-time allocation itself, and we show that it composes with inference-time methods across both text and video (Table~\ref{tab:dit-inference}).

\paragraph{Evaluation for long-context behavior.}
Long-context evaluation spans synthetic retrieval diagnostics and broader task suites \citep{bai2024longbench, hsieh2024ruler}, with prior work showing that long contexts can fail even when relevant information is nominally present \citep{liu2024lost}. We use both PE-dominant settings (isolating frequency-allocation effects) and retrieval-heavy evaluation (testing systems-level claims), treating incomplete larger-scale evidence conservatively.
