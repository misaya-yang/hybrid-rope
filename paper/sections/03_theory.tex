The surrogate is functional (not pointwise: Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation} reports $24$--$92\%$ collision-score reduction under the exact kernel across $12$ configurations). The pure-tether branch of the stationary ODE (Theorem~\ref{thm:ode}) is a design choice that keeps the allocation invertible in closed form; it is not a claim that the Fisher-driven forcing term never matters. The rule $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport}) is an operating-point selector whose $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent are derived; its remaining $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale is fixed by normalization convention, and its role is to move the allocation away from the $\tau{=}0$ boundary into a wide non-geometric basin.

\subsection{RoPE as frequency allocation}

Let $\phi \in [0,1]$ parameterize log-frequency and define $\omega(\phi) = b^{-\phi}$. A discrete RoPE design is equivalent to choosing a density $\rho(\phi)$ over this interval and discretizing it by inverse CDF (Figure~\ref{fig:method-overview}). Geometric RoPE corresponds to uniform $\rho$; EVQ derives $\rho$ from a variational objective. This reparameterization lifts the design space from a hand-written index rule to a continuous allocation problem.

\subsection{Exact kernel and broadband surrogate}

Under a distance prior $D(\Delta)$, the phase-collision kernel is
\begin{equation}
K(\phi_1,\phi_2)
=
\int D(\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_1)\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_2)\Delta)\,d\Delta.
\end{equation}
The collision objective admits a broadband functional surrogate,
\begin{equation}
K_{\mathrm{app}}(\phi_1,\phi_2)
\approx
\alpha \delta(\phi_1-\phi_2) + \beta \min(\phi_1,\phi_2),
\end{equation}
which isolates a diagonal ridge term and a smooth off-diagonal covariance. The $\delta$ term captures local ridge structure; $\min(\phi_1,\phi_2)$ is the Green kernel of a one-dimensional second-order operator. The exact kernel is oscillatory and cannot be pointwise-matched by a smooth two-parameter model; $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ captures the \emph{functional} structure of the collision objective. Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation} validates this: the EVQ solution derived from $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ reduces the collision score under the exact kernel by $24$--$92\%$ across 12 configurations, and a collision-only numerical oracle search (Appendix~\ref{sec:mechanism-isolation}) reaches comparable order-of-magnitude reductions, suggesting that non-cosh families may further reduce exact-kernel collision at the cost of losing closed-form invertibility. A self-consistency theorem (Theorem~\ref{thm:self-consistency} in Appendix) further confirms the surrogate's structural rigidity.

\subsection{Variational objective and stationary ODE}
\label{sec:ode}

The broadband functional is
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}[\rho]
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}\int \rho(\phi)^2\,d\phi
+
\frac{\beta}{2}\int\!\!\int \rho(\phi)\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\phi\,d\psi
-
\mu\int \rho(\phi)b^{-2\phi}\,d\phi.
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}[Stationary ODE]
\label{thm:ode}
Conditional on $K_{\mathrm{app}}$, the stationary density satisfies
\begin{equation}
\rho''(\phi) - \tau^2 \rho(\phi) = \gamma\, b^{-2\phi},
\qquad
\tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha},
\end{equation}
with a closed-form solution consisting of a hyperbolic tether and a Fisher-driven forcing term.
\end{theorem}

The ODE and its closed-form family are exact conditional on $K_{\mathrm{app}}$, with integration constants fixed by \emph{natural} boundary conditions from the $\min$-kernel Green structure (Appendix~\ref{sec:proofs})---not by anchoring endpoint frequencies. The decomposition separates a global tether (redistributing density) from a local Fisher-utility forcing term, ensuring EVQ is the stationary compromise rather than an unconstrained long-range distortion.

\subsection{Closed-form EVQ and the geometric limit}

The pure-tether branch yields an inverse-CDF warp
\begin{equation}
\phi_k(\tau)
=
1 - \frac{1}{\tau}
\operatorname{arcsinh}\!\left((1-u_k)\sinh\tau\right),
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\end{equation}
and inverse frequencies $b^{-\phi_k(\tau)}$.

\begin{theorem}[Geometric limit]
EVQ-cosh converges smoothly to geometric RoPE as $\tau \to 0$. Geometric RoPE is therefore the $\tau{=}0$ degenerate limit of the EVQ family, not a generic optimum.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Practical EVQ instantiation}
\label{sec:evq-practical}

For a head dimension $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ and training length $L$, the complete implementation is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:evq-practical}
\tau = \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{\sqrt{L}},
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\qquad
\omega_k^{\mathrm{EVQ}} = b^{-\phi_k(\tau)},
\qquad
K=\frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{2}.
\end{equation}
The construction is deliberately minimal: set $\tau$, compute quantiles, evaluate the warp, replace inverse frequencies. No auxiliary loss, no extra parameters, no optimizer dependence. The endpoint grid $u_k{=}k/K$ has the anchoring property $u_0{=}0{\Rightarrow}\phi_0{=}0$ (preserves $\omega_0{=}1$); our implementation uses midpoint quantization $u_k{=}(2k{+}1)/(2K)$, which trades exact anchoring for avoiding endpoint cells. We do not rely on $\omega_0{=}1$ in any trained model, and the endpoint/midpoint choice affects PPL by ${<}1\%$ in the $<1\%$ PPL basin across all $K{\geq}16$ settings we tested.

EVQ-cosh changes only the inverse-frequency initialization---distinct from learnable PE approaches that rely on in-distribution training dynamics to discover the allocation through extra parameters. The full recipe is Equation~(\ref{eq:evq-practical}) above plus $\tau{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L_{\mathrm{train}}}$: zero learned parameters, architecture unchanged.

\subsection{Waterbed inequality}
\label{sec:waterbed}

Under the broadband surrogate, EVQ obeys a tight waterbed inequality (Proposition~\ref{prop:waterbed}, Appendix~\ref{sec:waterbed-proof}): the error functional $\mathcal{W}(\rho) = \alpha\chi^2(U\|P_\rho) + \beta\!\int M_\rho^2$ satisfies $\mathcal{W}(\rho) \ge \alpha(e^{D_B(\rho)} - 1)$ with equality iff $\rho\equiv 1$, and EVQ's in-range cost scales as $\mathcal{W}(\rho_{\tau_*}) = O(d_{\mathrm{head}}^4/(L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J})^2)$. Long-range improvement is thus paid for by a bounded, quartic-in-$\tau^*$ in-range cost---not eliminated.

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\tau^*$}{tau*} scaling law: epistemic stratification}
\label{sec:tau-star}

\paragraph{Shape and scale come from separate layers.} The cosh shape is the pre-softmax variational optimum under $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ (\S\ref{sec:ode}--\S\ref{sec:evq-practical}); the scale $\tau^*$ is then selected \emph{post}-softmax by the following proposition, whose $1/L$ factor is the softmax Jacobian eigenvalue at the diffuse long-range baseline $p_0{=}1/L$---an algebraic property of softmax, not a CLT/i.i.d.\ claim about token sequences.

\begin{proposition}[Softmax Transport Temperature Selection]
\label{prop:softmax-transport}
Define $\mathcal{F}(\tau) = S_{\chi^2}(\tau) - \lambda\,U(\tau, L)$,
where $S_{\chi^2}(\tau)$ is the Pearson~$\chi^2$ stiffness and $U(\tau,L) = (d_{\mathrm{head}}/L)\int_0^1 q(Lb^{-\phi})\rho_\tau(\phi)d\phi$ is the post-softmax transport utility, $q(x) = 1/2 + \sin(2x)/(4x) - (\sin x/x)^2$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}). The $1/L$ factor arises from the softmax Jacobian at $p_0 = 1/L$; $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ is the per-head normalization shared by stiffness and utility.
In the small-$\tau$ limit, the minimizer satisfies
\[
\tau_*^2 \;=\; 45\,\lambda\,Q_1(L,b)\cdot\frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}^2}{L},
\qquad
Q_1(L,b) \;=\; \int_0^1 \eta(\phi)\,q(Lb^{-\phi})\,d\phi,
\]
with $\eta(\phi) = (1-\phi)^2/2 - 1/6$, hence $\tau_* \propto d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ with $L$-exponent exactly $-1/2$.
\end{proposition}

\paragraph{Non-diffuse baseline and $L_{\mathrm{eff}}$.}
\label{sec:leff-remark}
The $1/L$ factor used $p_0{=}1/L$. Under a trained attention distribution the corresponding scalar is $1/L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$, the reciprocal softmax-Jacobian Rayleigh quotient on the RoPE-induced logit transport direction $g_i$; $g_i$ has a closed form~\eqref{eq:g-closed-form} and $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ is defined as a ratio-of-means~\eqref{eq:leff-ratio-of-means} (Appendix~\ref{sec:leff-derivation}). This gives $\tau^{*}\propto d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}}$, and writing $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}{=}\Theta(L_{\mathrm{train}}^{a})$ unifies the rule as $\tau^{*}\propto L_{\mathrm{train}}^{-a/2}$, with $a{\approx}1$ on our $L\!\le\!8$K grid. A cheap entropy proxy $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{H}{=}(\mathbb{E}[e^{-H}])^{-1}$ agrees with $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ under flat-window attention but diverges at near-one-hot heads (where softmax saturates), so we use $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{H}$ only as a diagnostic. Saturation ($a\!\to\!0$) is empirically testable and would revise $\tau^{*}$ at $L_{\mathrm{train}}{\geq}16$K.

\paragraph{Three epistemic tiers.} The $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ and $L^{-1/2}$ structure is \textbf{derived} (exact in the small-$\tau$ limit; at practical $\tau$ numerical optimization yields $\gamma \approx 0.465$, within $1\%$ PPL of $L^{-0.500}$). The $\chi^2$ stiffness is \textbf{first-principles under a channel-load axiom}: with channels sampled according to $\rho\,d\phi$ and local cell width $\ell(\phi){=}1/\rho(\phi)$, $S_{\chi^2}(\rho) = \mathrm{Var}_{\rho}(1/\rho)$, i.e.\ the variance of per-channel quantization load (Appendix~\ref{sec:chi2-load}). Among load-moment penalties $S_p(\rho){=}\mathbb{E}_\rho[(1/\rho-1)^p]$ only $p{=}2$ reproduces $L^{-1/2}$; smooth $f$-divergences (KL, Jeffreys, R\'enyi) are locally quadratic around $\rho{=}1$ and agree with $\chi^2$ at leading order, but their \emph{finite-$\tau$} best points over our $L$ grid give $L$-exponents $\gamma\in\{0.58\mbox{--}0.81\}$---outside the $\pm 1\%$ PPL basin around $\gamma{=}0.500$ at the tested scales (Table~\ref{tab:stiffness-sweep}). The Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ is an $\mathcal{O}(1)$ \textbf{curvature-ratio} between the surrogate and exact utilities (Appendix~\ref{sec:lambda-curvature}); the collision-score oracle yields $c_{\mathrm{coll}} = 1.171 \pm 0.001$ (CV $0.11\%$, 9 configurations; Table~\ref{tab:lambda-cv}), which we read as a $17\%$ surrogate curvature under-estimate rather than as a free fit parameter; using $\lambda{=}1$ remains inside the ${<}1\%$ PPL basin across all 27 configurations.

\paragraph{Basin, not point estimate; additive corrections.} With $\lambda{=}1$, the formula falls within $<1\%$ PPL of the best observed sweep points across 27 configurations (MHA 50M--750M, MLA, video DiT, post-hoc LoRA at 7B; Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-scaling}): a wide near-optimal basin surrounds the variational solution while the uniform $\tau{=}0$ baseline sits outside it, so the derivation identifies the \emph{direction}, not a perfect $\tau$. EVQ corrects \emph{shape} while YaRN corrects \emph{range} (Figure~\ref{fig:orthogonal}); the corrections are additive, predicting the strong EVQ+YaRN composition observed in \S\ref{sec:experiments}, and attention-distance measurements verify the waterbed crossover at ${\approx}508$ tokens (Figure~\ref{fig:attn-mechanism}, Appendix).
