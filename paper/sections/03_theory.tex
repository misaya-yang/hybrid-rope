We do not claim an analytic solution to the full trained-transformer objective or direct optimization of the exact oscillatory RoPE kernel. The theorem-level analytic claim is conditional on the broadband surrogate $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$: its Green-kernel relaxation yields a convex variational problem with an elementary inverse-CDF minimizer. The surrogate is validated functionally, not pointwise (Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation}: $24$--$92\%$ collision-score reduction across $12$ configs); downstream PPL/passkey measures trained-model consequences. The pure-tether branch (Theorem~\ref{thm:ode}) is a closed-form design choice, not a claim that Fisher forcing vanishes. The rule $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport}) is an operating-point selector with convention-dependent $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale, not a global optimum.

\subsection{RoPE as frequency allocation}

Let $\phi \in [0,1]$ parameterize log-frequency, $\omega(\phi) = b^{-\phi}$. Let $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ denote the number of dimensions to which RoPE is applied and $K = d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ the number of rotary frequency pairs. For standard MHA, $d_{\mathrm{rot}} = d_{\mathrm{head}}$; for compressed-RoPE architectures such as MLA (\S\ref{sec:exp-mla}), $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ may be smaller than the full attention head dimension $d_{\mathrm{head}}$, in which case the inverse-CDF grid uses $K = d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ while the operating-point scale uses the effective attention-head dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ specified for that architecture (we take $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$ for MLA; see \S\ref{sec:exp-mla} and Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). We use a positive probability density $\rho \in C^2([0,1])$ with $\int_0^1\rho(\phi)\,d\phi = 1$ to parameterize a family of discrete RoPE allocations via inverse-CDF quantization: $\phi_k = F_\rho^{-1}(u_k)$ with $F_\rho(\phi) = \int_0^\phi\rho(s)\,ds$ and grid $u_k$ (Figure~\ref{fig:method-overview}). Geometric RoPE corresponds to $\rho \equiv 1$.

\subsection{A surrogate collision functional}

Under a smooth, bounded-support distance prior $D \in W^{m,1}(\mathbb{R})$ ($m\geq 1$; the regime relevant to autoregressive language models with finite effective context), the exact phase-collision kernel is $K(\phi_1,\phi_2) = \int D(\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_1)\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_2)\Delta)\,d\Delta$; it is oscillatory and is not pointwise approximated by a smooth two-term model. We therefore introduce the following surrogate collision quadratic form as the object of our variational analysis:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}[\rho]
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}\!\int_0^1\! \rho(\phi)^2\,d\phi
+
\frac{\beta}{2}\!\iint_{[0,1]^2}\! \rho(\phi)\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\phi\,d\psi,
\label{eq:Capp}
\end{equation}
with constants $\alpha,\beta>0$ \emph{fitted from the exact kernel $K$ on the deployed discrete channel grid} ($K{\in}\{16,32\}$; Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-scaling}), giving $\alpha{\approx}1/d_{\mathrm{rot}}$. The diagonal $\alpha\!\int\!\rho^2$ penalizes channel-load concentration; $\min(\phi,\psi)$ is the Green kernel of $-\partial_\phi^2$ on $[0,1]$ and encodes cumulative low-frequency overlap. $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ is a discrete-grid surrogate, not the continuum stationary-phase asymptotic of $K$ (which would give a $\phi$-dependent diagonal coefficient; see Appendix~\ref{sec:why-constant-alpha}). We validate $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ \emph{functionally}: allocations derived from it reduce the exact-kernel collision score by $24$--$92\%$ across $12$ configurations, and a collision-only oracle search reaches comparable reductions (Appendices~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation},~\ref{sec:mechanism-isolation}).

\subsection{Constrained stationary allocation}
\label{sec:ode}

Minimizing $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}[\rho]$ in~\eqref{eq:Capp} subject to $\int_0^1\rho(\phi)\,d\phi = 1$ with Lagrange multiplier $\nu$, the first variation gives $\alpha\rho(\phi) + \beta\,g(\phi) + \nu = 0$ where $g(\phi) = \int_0^1\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\psi$. Since $g''(\phi) = -\rho(\phi)$, every interior stationary density satisfies the homogeneous equation $\rho''(\phi) - \tau^2\rho(\phi) = 0$ with $\tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha}$. The pre-differentiated stationarity and mass normalization give the derivative boundary conditions $\rho'(0) = -\tau^2$ and $\rho'(1) = 0$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:proofs}).

\begin{theorem}[Exact stationary allocation under the broadband surrogate]
\label{thm:ode}
Under~\eqref{eq:Capp} with $\alpha{>}0,\beta{\geq}0$ (so $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ is convex; Appendix~\ref{sec:proofs}) and constraints $\rho\in C^2([0,1])$, $\rho{>}0$, $\int_0^1\rho{=}1$, the unique constrained minimizer is
\begin{equation}
\rho_\tau(\phi) \;=\; \frac{\tau\,\cosh(\tau(1{-}\phi))}{\sinh\tau}, \qquad \tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha},
\end{equation}
strictly positive on $[0,1]$ for $\beta{>}0$; at $\beta{=}0$ the minimizer is the $\tau{\to}0$ limit $\rho_0\equiv 1$ (uniform/geometric).
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Fisher-forcing extension (Appendix).} Adding a Fisher utility $-\mu\!\int\rho\,b^{-2\phi}\,d\phi$ to~\eqref{eq:Capp} changes the stationary equation to $\rho'' - \tau^2\rho = \gamma\,b^{-2\phi}$, whose non-resonant solution includes a particular term $\gamma\,b^{-2\phi}/((2\log b)^2-\tau^2)$. EVQ-Cosh corresponds to the homogeneous $\gamma{=}0$ branch; the forced extension is analyzed as a perturbation in Appendix~\ref{sec:pure-tether-bound}. Our practical method uses the homogeneous branch because it gives a closed-form invertible CDF; the forced branch is a controlled but nonzero residual, not zero pointwise.

\subsection{Closed-form EVQ and the geometric limit}

The pure-tether branch yields an inverse-CDF warp
\begin{equation}
\phi_k(\tau)
=
1 - \frac{1}{\tau}
\operatorname{arcsinh}\!\left((1-u_k)\sinh\tau\right),
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\end{equation}
and inverse frequencies $b^{-\phi_k(\tau)}$.

\begin{theorem}[Closed-form pure-tether allocation and geometric limit]
For $\tau > 0$, the stationary density in Theorem~\ref{thm:ode} (the homogeneous, pure-tether branch) has CDF $F_\tau(\phi) = 1 - \sinh(\tau(1{-}\phi))/\sinh\tau$, so inverse-CDF quantization gives $\phi_k(\tau) = 1 - \tau^{-1}\operatorname{arcsinh}((1{-}u_k)\sinh\tau)$. As $\tau\to 0$, $\phi_k(\tau) \to u_k$ and geometric RoPE is recovered \emph{within the pure-tether sub-family}; the Fisher-forced extension (Appendix~\ref{subsec:fisher-ext}) does not vanish in this limit.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Practical EVQ instantiation}
\label{sec:evq-practical}

For an effective attention-head dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and training length $L$, the complete implementation is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:evq-practical}
\tau = \frac{d_{\mathrm{eff}}}{\sqrt{L}},
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\qquad
\omega_k^{\mathrm{EVQ}} = b^{-\phi_k(\tau)},
\qquad
K=\frac{d_{\mathrm{rot}}}{2}.
\end{equation}
For standard MHA, $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{rot}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$; for compressed-RoPE MLA, $K{=}d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ counts only the rotary channels while $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ follows the architecture-specific convention (we take $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$; see \S\ref{sec:exp-mla} and Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). The construction is deliberately minimal: set $\tau$, compute quantiles, evaluate the warp, replace inverse frequencies. No auxiliary loss, no extra parameters, no optimizer dependence. The endpoint grid $u_k{=}k/K$ has the anchoring property $u_0{=}0{\Rightarrow}\phi_0{=}0$ (preserves $\omega_0{=}1$); our implementation uses midpoint quantization $u_k{=}(2k{+}1)/(2K)$, which trades exact anchoring for avoiding endpoint cells. We do not rely on $\omega_0{=}1$ in any trained model, and the endpoint/midpoint choice shifts PPL by ${<}1\%$ across all $K{\geq}16$ settings we tested, well inside the flat empirical basin.

EVQ-Cosh changes only the inverse-frequency initialization---distinct from learnable PE approaches that rely on in-distribution training dynamics to discover the allocation through extra parameters. The full recipe is Equation~\eqref{eq:evq-practical} plus $\tau{=}d_{\mathrm{eff}}/\sqrt{L_{\mathrm{train}}}$: zero learned parameters, architecture unchanged.

\subsection{Waterbed inequality}
\label{sec:waterbed}

Under the broadband surrogate, EVQ obeys a waterbed inequality (a corollary of the sharper envelope in Proposition~\ref{prop:waterbed}, Appendix~\ref{sec:waterbed-proof}). Writing $P_\rho$ for the CDF-induced measure and $U$ for the uniform distribution on $[0,1]$, and letting $D_{\mathrm{Burg}}(\rho){:=}{-}\!\int_0^1\!\log\rho(\phi)\,d\phi$ denote the Burg entropy deficit relative to uniform, the Pearson-divergence companion functional $\mathcal{W}(\rho) = \alpha\chi^2(U\|P_\rho) + \beta\!\int M_\rho^2$ (related to but distinct from $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$: both vanish at $\rho{\equiv}1$ with leading-order $\mathcal{O}(\tau^4)$ growth; they diverge at large $\tau$) satisfies $\mathcal{W}(\rho) \ge \alpha(e^{D_{\mathrm{Burg}}(\rho)} - 1)$ with equality iff $\rho\equiv 1$. Since $\mathcal{W}(\rho_\tau){=}\mathcal{O}(\tau^4)$ and Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport} (with the $L\!\to\!L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ substitution from Appendix~\ref{sec:leff-derivation}) gives $\tau_*^2{\sim}d_{\mathrm{head}}^2/L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$, EVQ's in-range Pearson deviation scales as $\mathcal{W}(\rho_{\tau_*}) = \mathcal{O}(d_{\mathrm{head}}^4/(L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J})^2)$. Long-range improvement is thus paid for by a bounded, quartic-in-$\tau^*$ in-range cost.

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\tau^*$}{tau*} scaling law: epistemic stratification}
\label{sec:tau-star}

\paragraph{Shape and scale come from separate layers.} The cosh shape is the pre-softmax stationary solution under $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ (\S\ref{sec:ode}--\S\ref{sec:evq-practical}); the scale $\tau^*$ is selected \emph{post}-softmax by the proposition below, with $U(\tau,L)$ the per-channel post-softmax KL gain from an $\mathcal{O}(\tau^2)$ RoPE logit perturbation (formal definition Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}). The $1/L$ factor is the softmax Jacobian eigenvalue at the diffuse baseline $p_0{=}1/L$---an algebraic property, not a CLT claim; convention $\tfrac{1}{2}$ factors from the KL Taylor expansion are absorbed into $\lambda$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:lambda-curvature}).

\begin{proposition}[Semi-analytic operating-point scaling under a diffuse baseline]
\label{prop:softmax-transport}
On the pure-tether family $\rho_\tau$, and assuming a diffuse post-softmax baseline $p_0{=}1/L$, the Pearson-$\chi^2$ channel-load stiffness and post-softmax transport utility admit small-$\tau$ expansions
\[
S_{\chi^2}(\tau) \;=\; c_S\,\frac{\tau^4}{d_{\mathrm{head}}} + O(\tau^6), \quad c_S{=}\tfrac{1}{45},
\qquad
U(\tau,L) \;=\; \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{L}\bigl[Q_0 + \tau^2 Q_1(L,b) + O(\tau^4)\bigr],
\]
with $Q_1(L,b) = \int_0^1 \eta(\phi)\,q(Lb^{-\phi})\,d\phi$, $\eta(\phi) = (1{-}\phi)^2/2{-}1/6$, and $q(x) = 1/2 + \sin(2x)/(4x) - (\sin x/x)^2$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}). The stationary point of $\mathcal{F}(\tau) = \tfrac{1}{2}S_{\chi^2}(\tau) - \lambda\,U(\tau,L)$ therefore satisfies
\[
\tau_*^2 \;=\; 45\,\lambda\,Q_1(L,b)\cdot\frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}^2}{L},
\qquad \tau_* \;\propto\; \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{\sqrt{L}}.
\]
The $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent follow from the combined small-$\tau$ scalings; the rule balances an $\mathcal{O}(\tau^2)$ utility gain (RoPE logit perturbation transported through a diffuse softmax with $1/L$ Jacobian curvature) against an $\mathcal{O}(\tau^4)$ channel-load stiffness. Real $\tau_*$ requires $Q_1(L,b){>}0$, verified across the tested grid ($Q_1{\in}[0.008, 0.032]$; Appendix~\ref{sec:lambda-cv}). The remaining $\mathcal{O}(1)$ prefactor depends on normalization convention and is fixed empirically inside a flat basin, not claimed as a global optimum.
\end{proposition}

\paragraph{Three epistemic tiers.}
\label{sec:leff-remark}
The small-$\tau$ expansion in Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport} \emph{derives the structural} $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent; at practical $\tau$ (e.g., $\tau{=}4$ in the PE-dominant setting) the exact cosh family is used directly, not the Taylor truncation, and numerical optimization yields an exponent within $1\%$ PPL of $L^{-0.500}$. The Pearson $\chi^2$ stiffness $S_{\chi^2}(\rho){=}\mathrm{Var}_{\rho d\phi}(1/\rho)$ is the canonical load-variance penalty under inverse-CDF cell-width $1/\rho(\phi)$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:chi2-load}); the Lagrange multiplier $\lambda{=}1$ is a unit convention, with an exact-kernel sweep giving $c_{\mathrm{coll}}{=}1.171$ vs.\ surrogate $\sqrt{45Q_1}{\approx}1.19$ ($1.6\%$ relative, CV $0.28\%$, 9 configs; Table~\ref{tab:lambda-cv}). For trained, non-diffuse attention the same expansion replaces $1/L$ by a softmax-Jacobian Rayleigh quotient $1/L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:leff-derivation}); we register direct measurement at $L_{\mathrm{train}}{\geq}16$K as a falsifiable test of the diffuse-baseline approximation.

\paragraph{Basin, not point estimate.} With $\lambda{=}1$, the formula falls within $<1\%$ PPL of the best observed sweep points across 27 configurations; pure geometric ($\tau{=}0$) is the natural negative control and falls $10$--$46\%$ outside (Appendices~\ref{sec:tau-scaling},~\ref{sec:lambda-cv}). The bare formula stays inside this basin for our primary $b{\geq}500\mathrm{K}$ regime; at small $b$ and large $L$ the explicit $c_{\mathrm{pred}}(L,b){=}\sqrt{45\,Q_1(L,b)}$ form is recommended. EVQ and YaRN correct orthogonal deficiencies (shape vs.\ range; Appendix Fig.~\ref{fig:orthogonal}), motivating the EVQ+YaRN composition test in \S\ref{sec:experiments}.
