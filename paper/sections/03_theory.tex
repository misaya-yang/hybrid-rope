\subsection{RoPE as frequency allocation}

Let \(\phi \in [0,1]\) parameterize log-frequency and define \(\omega(\phi) = b^{-\phi}\). A discrete RoPE design is equivalent to choosing a density \(\rho(\phi)\) over this interval and discretizing it by inverse CDF. Standard geometric RoPE corresponds to uniform \(\rho\); EVQ derives \(\rho\) from a variational objective. This reparameterization lifts the design space from a hand-written index rule to a continuous allocation problem.

\subsection{Exact kernel and broadband surrogate}

Under a distance prior \(D(\Delta)\), the phase-collision kernel is
\begin{equation}
K(\phi_1,\phi_2)
=
\int D(\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_1)\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_2)\Delta)\,d\Delta.
\end{equation}
Our only approximation step is a broadband surrogate,
\begin{equation}
K_{\mathrm{app}}(\phi_1,\phi_2)
\approx
\alpha \delta(\phi_1-\phi_2) + \beta \min(\phi_1,\phi_2),
\end{equation}
which isolates a diagonal ridge term and a smooth off-diagonal covariance term. The \(\delta\) term captures local ridge structure, while \(\min(\phi_1,\phi_2)\) is the Green kernel of a one-dimensional second-order operator. After this step, the remaining derivation is exact. The exact kernel is oscillatory (sinc-like) and cannot be well-approximated pointwise by a smooth two-parameter model; instead, $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ captures the \emph{functional} structure of the collision objective. Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation} validates this across 12 configurations spanning three orders of magnitude in $L$ and $b$: the EVQ solution derived from $K_{\mathrm{app}}$ reduces the collision score under the exact kernel by $24$--$92\%$ in every tested regime.

\subsection{Variational objective and exact ODE}

The broadband functional takes the form
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}[\rho]
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}\int \rho(\phi)^2\,d\phi
+
\frac{\beta}{2}\int\!\!\int \rho(\phi)\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\phi\,d\psi
-
\mu\int \rho(\phi)b^{-2\phi}\,d\phi.
\end{equation}

\begin{theorem}
After fixing the surrogate \(K_{\mathrm{app}}\), the stationary density satisfies
\begin{equation}
\rho''(\phi) - \tau^2 \rho(\phi) = \gamma\, b^{-2\phi},
\qquad
\tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha},
\end{equation}
with a closed-form solution consisting of a hyperbolic tether and a Fisher-driven forcing term.
\end{theorem}

The surrogate is the only approximation; the ODE and its closed-form family are exact conditional on $K_{\mathrm{app}}$. The decomposition separates a global tether (redistributing density) from a local Fisher-utility forcing term, ensuring EVQ is the stationary compromise rather than an unconstrained long-range distortion.

\subsection{Closed-form EVQ and the geometric limit}

The pure-tether branch yields an inverse-CDF warp
\begin{equation}
\phi_k(\tau)
=
1 - \frac{1}{\tau}
\operatorname{arcsinh}\!\left((1-u_k)\sinh\tau\right),
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\end{equation}
and inverse frequencies \(b^{-\phi_k(\tau)}\).

\begin{theorem}
EVQ-cosh converges smoothly to geometric RoPE as \(\tau \to 0\). Therefore, geometric RoPE is the \(\tau=0\) degenerate limit of the EVQ family rather than a generic optimum.
\end{theorem}

EVQ changes only the inverse-frequency initialization---the key distinction from learnable PE approaches, which rely on in-distribution training dynamics to discover the allocation through extra parameters.

\subsection{Practical EVQ instantiation}

For a head dimension \(d_{\mathrm{head}}\) and training length \(L\), the implementation used throughout the paper is
\begin{equation}
\tau = \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{\sqrt{L}},
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\qquad
\omega_k^{\mathrm{EVQ}} = b^{-\phi_k(\tau)},
\qquad
K=\frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{2}.
\end{equation}
The construction is deliberately minimal: four deterministic steps (set $\tau$, compute quantiles, evaluate the warp, replace inverse frequencies) with no auxiliary loss, no extra parameters, and no optimizer dependence. Two properties follow from the quantile form: $u_0{=}0$ implies $\phi_0(\tau){=}0$, so the first channel stays anchored at $\omega_0{=}1$; and because the warp grows toward the low-frequency end, EVQ automatically preserves short-range structure without a separate design choice.

\subsection{Waterbed inequality}

\begin{proposition}
Under the assumptions used in the broadband model, EVQ obeys a waterbed inequality: improving long-range frequency resolution necessarily redistributes error rather than eliminating it globally.
\end{proposition}

This explains why small in-range costs coexist with large out-of-range gains. Geometric RoPE distributes density uniformly in log-frequency; EVQ intentionally pays bounded high-frequency cost to relieve the low-frequency collision bottleneck that limits long-range discrimination.

\subsection{Optimal temperature and the \texorpdfstring{$\tau^*$}{tau*} scaling law}

The broadband surrogate provides the $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ dependence ($\alpha \approx 1/d_{\mathrm{head}}$, so $\sqrt{\beta/\alpha} \propto \sqrt{d_{\mathrm{head}}}$) but predicts only $L^{-0.11}$, too weak by a factor of~5. The missing $L$-dependence originates from the softmax attention map.

\begin{proposition}[Softmax Transport Temperature Selection]
\label{prop:softmax-transport}
Define $\mathcal{F}(\tau) = S_{\chi^2}(\tau) - \lambda\,U(\tau, L)$,
where $S_{\chi^2}(\tau) = \frac{1}{M}\!\left[\frac{\sinh\tau\cdot\arctan(\sinh\tau)}{\tau^2} - 1\right]$ is the Pearson~$\chi^2$ stiffness and $U(\tau,L) = \frac{M}{L}\int_0^1 q(Lb^{-\phi})\rho_\tau(\phi)\,d\phi$ is the post-softmax transport utility. The $1/L$ factor arises from the softmax Jacobian at diffuse baseline $p_0 = 1/L$.
Then the minimizer satisfies $\tau_* \propto d_{\mathrm{head}}/L^{\gamma}$ with $\gamma \in [0.47, 0.51]$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}).
\end{proposition}

The $\chi^2$ stiffness reflects attention-load asymmetry: diluted channels ($\rho < 1$) bear per-channel cost ${\propto}1/\rho$. KL, Jeffreys, and all R\'{e}nyi divergences produce $L$-exponents far from $-0.5$; only the $\chi^2$ family succeeds (Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}, Table~\ref{tab:stiffness-sweep}). The resulting scaling law
\begin{equation}
\tau^*(L) = \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{\sqrt{L}}
\end{equation}
is semi-analytic: $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ dependence and $\gamma$ are derived, but $\lambda$ is calibrated empirically. Validation across 99 runs (27 configurations, Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-scaling}) confirms a shallow optimality basin ($<1\%$ PPL gap), making the formula a robust default. When $L$ is short, $\tau^*$ is large (PE-dominant regime); as $L$ grows, $\tau^*$ shrinks, predicting bounded in-range cost.

\subsection{Predictions}

The theory predicts an asymmetric tradeoff: EVQ redistributes channel density, expanding low-frequency spacing while compressing high-frequency spacing. Short-range costs remain bounded (high-frequency channels are redundant for nearby positions), while long-range gains can be large (the low-frequency collision bottleneck is the dominant failure mode). The same picture predicts a base dependence: EVQ should help most when the collision block is large (high base, short $L$) and help less when it is already small.

Critically, the theory predicts \emph{complementarity} with inference-time scaling. Geometric RoPE has two orthogonal deficiencies: a \emph{shape} deficiency (uniform density wastes channels) and a \emph{range} deficiency (frequencies designed for $L_{\mathrm{train}}$ cannot cover longer distances). EVQ addresses shape; inference-time methods such as YaRN address range. Figure~\ref{fig:orthogonal} makes this explicit: in log-frequency space, EVQ shifts $\phi_k$ \emph{below} the geometric diagonal while YaRN shifts it \emph{above}. The corrections are additive, explaining why the combination outperforms either intervention alone.

The theory also predicts that EVQ should \emph{not} help when the collision-block analysis predicts little room for improvement; the low-base dead-zone experiments confirm this (Appendix). Direct measurement of attention distance distributions on a trained 750M model verifies the waterbed prediction: a crossover at ${\approx}508$ tokens separates a short-range regime (Geometric dominant) from a long-range regime (EVQ dominant, up to $2.5\times$ more attention mass; Figure~\ref{fig:attn-mechanism} in Appendix).
