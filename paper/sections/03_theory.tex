The surrogate is functional (not pointwise: Appendix~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation} reports $24$--$92\%$ collision-score reduction under the exact kernel across $12$ configurations). The pure-tether branch of the stationary ODE (Theorem~\ref{thm:ode}) is a design choice that keeps the allocation invertible in closed form; it is not a claim that the Fisher-driven forcing term never matters. The rule $\tau^{*}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}/\sqrt{L}$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport}) is an operating-point selector whose $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent are derived; its remaining $\mathcal{O}(1)$ scale is fixed by normalization convention, and its role is to move the allocation away from the $\tau{=}0$ boundary into a wide non-geometric basin.

\subsection{RoPE as frequency allocation}

Let $\phi \in [0,1]$ parameterize log-frequency, $\omega(\phi) = b^{-\phi}$. Let $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ denote the number of dimensions to which RoPE is applied and $K = d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ the number of rotary frequency pairs. For standard MHA, $d_{\mathrm{rot}} = d_{\mathrm{head}}$; for compressed-RoPE architectures such as MLA (\S\ref{sec:exp-mla}), $d_{\mathrm{rot}}$ may be smaller than the full attention head dimension $d_{\mathrm{head}}$, in which case the inverse-CDF grid uses $K = d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ while the operating-point scale uses the effective attention-head dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ specified for that architecture (we take $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$ for MLA; see \S\ref{sec:exp-mla} and Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). We use a positive probability density $\rho \in C^2([0,1])$ with $\int_0^1\rho(\phi)\,d\phi = 1$ to parameterize a family of discrete RoPE allocations via inverse-CDF quantization: $\phi_k = F_\rho^{-1}(u_k)$ with $F_\rho(\phi) = \int_0^\phi\rho(s)\,ds$ and grid $u_k$ (Figure~\ref{fig:method-overview}). Geometric RoPE corresponds to $\rho \equiv 1$.

\subsection{A surrogate collision functional}

Under a distance prior $D(\Delta)$, the exact phase-collision kernel is $K(\phi_1,\phi_2) = \int D(\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_1)\Delta)\cos(\omega(\phi_2)\Delta)\,d\Delta$; it is oscillatory and is not pointwise approximated by a smooth two-term model. We therefore introduce the following surrogate collision quadratic form as the object of our variational analysis:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}[\rho]
=
\frac{\alpha}{2}\!\int_0^1\! \rho(\phi)^2\,d\phi
+
\frac{\beta}{2}\!\iint_{[0,1]^2}\! \rho(\phi)\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\phi\,d\psi,
\label{eq:Capp}
\end{equation}
formally corresponding to the distributional kernel $K_{\mathrm{app}}(\phi,\psi) := \alpha\,\delta(\phi{-}\psi) + \beta\min(\phi,\psi)$. The diagonal term penalizes channel-load concentration; $\min(\phi,\psi)$ is the Green kernel of $-\partial_\phi^2$ on $[0,1]$ and encodes cumulative low-frequency overlap. We validate $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ \emph{functionally}, not pointwise: allocations derived from $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ reduce the exact-kernel collision score by $24$--$92\%$ across $12$ configurations, and a collision-only oracle search reaches comparable reductions (Appendices~\ref{sec:surrogate-validation},~\ref{sec:mechanism-isolation}).

\subsection{Constrained stationary allocation}
\label{sec:ode}

Minimizing $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}[\rho]$ in~\eqref{eq:Capp} subject to $\int_0^1\rho(\phi)\,d\phi = 1$ with Lagrange multiplier $\nu$, the first variation gives $\alpha\rho(\phi) + \beta\,g(\phi) + \nu = 0$ where $g(\phi) = \int_0^1\rho(\psi)\min(\phi,\psi)\,d\psi$. Since $g''(\phi) = -\rho(\phi)$, every interior stationary density satisfies the homogeneous equation $\rho''(\phi) - \tau^2\rho(\phi) = 0$ with $\tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha}$. The pre-differentiated stationarity and mass normalization give the derivative boundary conditions $\rho'(0) = -\tau^2$ and $\rho'(1) = 0$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:proofs}).

\begin{theorem}[Surrogate stationary allocation]
\label{thm:ode}
Under~\eqref{eq:Capp} and the constraints $\rho \in C^2([0,1])$, $\rho > 0$, $\int_0^1\rho = 1$, the unique positive interior stationary density is
\begin{equation}
\rho_\tau(\phi) \;=\; \frac{\tau\,\cosh(\tau(1{-}\phi))}{\sinh\tau}, \qquad \tau = \sqrt{\beta/\alpha}.
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\paragraph{Fisher-forcing extension (Appendix).} Adding a Fisher utility $-\mu\!\int\rho\,b^{-2\phi}\,d\phi$ to~\eqref{eq:Capp} changes the stationary equation to $\rho'' - \tau^2\rho = \gamma\,b^{-2\phi}$, whose non-resonant solution includes a particular term $\gamma\,b^{-2\phi}/((2\log b)^2-\tau^2)$. EVQ-Cosh corresponds to the homogeneous $\gamma{=}0$ branch; the forced extension is analyzed as a perturbation in Appendix~\ref{sec:pure-tether-bound}. Our practical method uses the homogeneous branch because it gives a closed-form invertible CDF; the forced branch is a controlled but nonzero residual, not zero pointwise.

\subsection{Closed-form EVQ and the geometric limit}

The pure-tether branch yields an inverse-CDF warp
\begin{equation}
\phi_k(\tau)
=
1 - \frac{1}{\tau}
\operatorname{arcsinh}\!\left((1-u_k)\sinh\tau\right),
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\end{equation}
and inverse frequencies $b^{-\phi_k(\tau)}$.

\begin{theorem}[Closed-form pure-tether allocation and geometric limit]
For $\tau > 0$, the stationary density in Theorem~\ref{thm:ode} has CDF $F_\tau(\phi) = 1 - \sinh(\tau(1{-}\phi))/\sinh\tau$, so inverse-CDF quantization gives $\phi_k(\tau) = 1 - \tau^{-1}\operatorname{arcsinh}((1{-}u_k)\sinh\tau)$. As $\tau\to 0$, $\phi_k(\tau) \to u_k$; geometric RoPE is thus recovered as the $\tau{\to}0$ limit of this pure-tether sub-family.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Practical EVQ instantiation}
\label{sec:evq-practical}

For an effective attention-head dimension $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ and training length $L$, the complete implementation is
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:evq-practical}
\tau = \frac{d_{\mathrm{eff}}}{\sqrt{L}},
\qquad
u_k = \frac{k}{K},
\qquad
\omega_k^{\mathrm{EVQ}} = b^{-\phi_k(\tau)},
\qquad
K=\frac{d_{\mathrm{rot}}}{2}.
\end{equation}
For standard MHA, $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{rot}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$; for compressed-RoPE MLA, $K{=}d_{\mathrm{rot}}/2$ counts only the rotary channels while $d_{\mathrm{eff}}$ follows the architecture-specific convention (we take $d_{\mathrm{eff}}{=}d_{\mathrm{head}}$; see \S\ref{sec:exp-mla} and Appendix~\ref{sec:mla-results}). The construction is deliberately minimal: set $\tau$, compute quantiles, evaluate the warp, replace inverse frequencies. No auxiliary loss, no extra parameters, no optimizer dependence. The endpoint grid $u_k{=}k/K$ has the anchoring property $u_0{=}0{\Rightarrow}\phi_0{=}0$ (preserves $\omega_0{=}1$); our implementation uses midpoint quantization $u_k{=}(2k{+}1)/(2K)$, which trades exact anchoring for avoiding endpoint cells. We do not rely on $\omega_0{=}1$ in any trained model, and the endpoint/midpoint choice shifts PPL by ${<}1\%$ across all $K{\geq}16$ settings we tested, well inside the flat empirical basin.

EVQ-Cosh changes only the inverse-frequency initialization---distinct from learnable PE approaches that rely on in-distribution training dynamics to discover the allocation through extra parameters. The full recipe is Equation~\eqref{eq:evq-practical} plus $\tau{=}d_{\mathrm{eff}}/\sqrt{L_{\mathrm{train}}}$: zero learned parameters, architecture unchanged.

\subsection{Waterbed inequality}
\label{sec:waterbed}

Under the broadband surrogate, EVQ obeys a waterbed inequality (a corollary of the sharper envelope in Proposition~\ref{prop:waterbed}, Appendix~\ref{sec:waterbed-proof}). Writing $P_\rho$ for the CDF-induced measure and $U$ for the uniform distribution on $[0,1]$, the error functional $\mathcal{W}(\rho) = \alpha\chi^2(U\|P_\rho) + \beta\!\int M_\rho^2$ satisfies $\mathcal{W}(\rho) \ge \alpha(e^{D_B(\rho)} - 1)$ with equality iff $\rho\equiv 1$, and EVQ's in-range surrogate cost scales as $\mathcal{W}(\rho_{\tau_*}) = O(d_{\mathrm{head}}^4/(L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J})^2)$. Long-range improvement is thus paid for by a bounded, quartic-in-$\tau^*$ in-range cost.

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$\tau^*$}{tau*} scaling law: epistemic stratification}
\label{sec:tau-star}

\paragraph{Shape and scale come from separate layers.} The cosh shape is the pre-softmax pure-tether stationary solution under $\mathcal{C}_{\mathrm{app}}$ (\S\ref{sec:ode}--\S\ref{sec:evq-practical}); the scale $\tau^*$ is then selected \emph{post}-softmax by the following proposition, whose $1/L$ factor is the softmax Jacobian eigenvalue at the diffuse long-range baseline $p_0{=}1/L$---an algebraic property of softmax, not a CLT/i.i.d.\ claim about token sequences.

\begin{proposition}[Small-$\tau$ operating-point scaling]
\label{prop:softmax-transport}
On the pure-tether family $\rho_\tau$, and assuming a diffuse post-softmax baseline $p_0{=}1/L$, the Pearson-$\chi^2$ channel-load stiffness and post-softmax transport utility admit small-$\tau$ expansions
\[
S_{\chi^2}(\tau) \;=\; c_S\,\frac{\tau^4}{d_{\mathrm{head}}} + O(\tau^6), \quad c_S{=}\tfrac{1}{45},
\qquad
U(\tau,L) \;=\; \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{L}\bigl[Q_0 + \tau^2 Q_1(L,b) + O(\tau^4)\bigr],
\]
with $Q_1(L,b) = \int_0^1 \eta(\phi)\,q(Lb^{-\phi})\,d\phi$, $\eta(\phi) = (1{-}\phi)^2/2{-}1/6$, and $q(x) = 1/2 + \sin(2x)/(4x) - (\sin x/x)^2$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:stiffness-derivation}). The stationary point of $\mathcal{F}(\tau) = \tfrac{1}{2}S_{\chi^2}(\tau) - \lambda\,U(\tau,L)$ therefore satisfies
\[
\tau_*^2 \;=\; 45\,\lambda\,Q_1(L,b)\cdot\frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}^2}{L},
\qquad \tau_* \;\propto\; \frac{d_{\mathrm{head}}}{\sqrt{L}}.
\]
The $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent follow from the combined small-$\tau$ scalings of $S_{\chi^2}$ and $U$; the remaining $\mathcal{O}(1)$ prefactor ($c_S$, $\lambda$, $Q_1$) depends on the normalization convention and is fixed empirically inside a flat basin (below), not claimed as a global optimum.
\end{proposition}

\paragraph{Non-diffuse baseline (diagnostic extension).}
\label{sec:leff-remark}
For trained, non-diffuse attention the same Taylor expansion replaces $1/L$ by a RoPE-directional softmax-Jacobian Rayleigh quotient $1/L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ (Appendix~\ref{sec:leff-derivation}); we treat this as a diagnostic extension of the diffuse-baseline calculation rather than as a main claim, and leave direct measurement of $L_{\mathrm{eff}}^{J}$ at longer contexts as a falsifiable check at $L_{\mathrm{train}}{\geq}16$K.

\paragraph{Three epistemic tiers.} The $d_{\mathrm{head}}$ factor and $L^{-1/2}$ exponent follow from the scalings in Proposition~\ref{prop:softmax-transport} under the stated small-$\tau$ assumptions; at practical $\tau$, numerical optimization yields an exponent within $1\%$ PPL of $L^{-0.500}$. We use the Pearson $\chi^2$ channel-load stiffness $S_{\chi^2}(\rho) = \mathrm{Var}_{\rho d\phi}(1/\rho)$ because inverse-CDF quantization assigns local cell width $\ell(\phi){=}1/\rho(\phi)$, so the variance of per-channel load is the canonical load-variance penalty (Appendix~\ref{sec:chi2-load}); alternative smooth divergences agree locally around $\rho\equiv 1$ but differ at finite $\tau$ over our grid (Table~\ref{tab:stiffness-sweep}) so we treat $\chi^2$ as the canonical motivated choice rather than a uniqueness claim. The Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$ fixes the unit convention for comparing stiffness and utility; we set $\lambda{=}1$ in deployment. The exact-kernel collision sweep (Table~\ref{tab:lambda-cv}) gives $c_{\mathrm{coll}}{=}1.171\pm 0.001$ against the surrogate prediction $c_{\mathrm{pred}}{=}\sqrt{45\,Q_1}{\approx}1.19$ (CV $0.28\%$, 9 configurations), an \emph{a posteriori} consistency check placing the convention inside the flat empirical basin rather than an independently derived constant.

\paragraph{Basin, not point estimate.} With $\lambda{=}1$, the formula falls within $<1\%$ PPL of the best observed sweep points across the tested grid of 27 configurations, with primary claims resting on 3-seed anchors (Appendix~\ref{sec:tau-scaling}). EVQ and YaRN correct orthogonal deficiencies (shape vs.\ range; Appendix Fig.~\ref{fig:orthogonal}), predicting the additive EVQ+YaRN composition in \S\ref{sec:experiments}; attention-distance measurements verify the waterbed crossover at ${\approx}508$ tokens (Appendix).
