# Abstract

Rotary Position Embedding (RoPE) has become the de-facto standard for injecting positional information into Large Language Models (LLMs). As the demand for processing extended context windows grows, numerous heuristic algorithms (e.g., Position Interpolation, YaRN, LongRoPE) have been proposed to stretch RoPE’s frequency manifold. However, these methods manipulate frequencies strictly as independent geometric variables, overlooking the continuous topological structure of language and occasionally inducing catastrophic degradation in mid-range retrieval tasks (Needle-In-A-Haystack).

In this paper, we establish that **there is no universally optimal RoPE frequency allocation independent of the data**. By taking the continuous thermodynamic limit of the frequency dimension, we bridge this theoretical gap mathematically. We formulate RoPE frequency modification as a rigoriously constrained Calculus of Variations problem. We prove that the optimal continuous frequency density measure $\rho^*(\phi)$ is the exact strict inverse of an energetic landscape dictated fundamentally by the empirical attention distance prior $\mathcal{D}(\Delta)$ of the training corpus. 

Through this unified continuous framework, we analytically reverse-engineer existing scaling behaviors: 
1) Standard geometric RoPE ($\rho=1$) is mathematically optimal if and only if the attention prior is strictly uniform; 
2) The ubiquitous natural language power-law prior ($\mathcal{D} \sim \Delta^{-1}$) necessitates a smoothly decaying convex density curve, with maximum deviation structurally bounded to $\mathcal{O}(\frac{\ln b}{\ln L})$; 
3) Ad-hoc heuristic manipulations that violate this continuous manifold arbitrarily trigger exponential spatial Fisher Information collapse, rigorously unearthing the "Waterbed Effect" responsible for proxy-metric traps and downstream fine-tuning failures.

Guided by these foundational theorems, we introduce **Anchored Hybrid RoPE**, a closed-form continuous frequency allocation that analytically satisfies the linguistic power-law prior while strictly preserving boundary representation capacity constraints. Evaluated across 50M, 100M, and 350M from-scratch pre-training runs, Anchored Hybrid inherently saturated the analytically predicted limits, achieving a stable $\sim 13.5\%$ improvement in context utilization without empirical hyperparameter searching. Furthermore, specialized isolated phase transition probes perfectly confirm our theoretically predicted crossover threshold. Our work provides the first end-to-end theoretical map bridging corpus distance priors and positional embedding capacity, formalizing LLM context scaling from an empirical art into a mathematically grounded science.
