# Limitations, Failures, and Discussion

## 1. The Necessity of Phase Adaptation (Training Dependency)
A significant limitation of our frequency re-shaping framework—and indeed of most non-linear positional interventions—is its strict reliance on gradient-based adaptation. Our ablation studies definitively show that zero-shot substitution of the RoPE phase manifold (e.g., swapping a geometric distribution for a sigmoid or hybrid curve at inference time without any fine-tuning) triggers massive relative distance discontinuities. Because the original self-attention weights perfectly overfit the pre-trained phase landscape, the model's perplexity diverges immediately upon substitution. Consequently, our proposed Anchored Hybrid allocations demand, at minimum, a short "Phase 4" continual pre-training phase or a LoRA alignment stage to teach the attention blocks the new phase distances.

## 2. The Base Selection Sensitivity and Fisher Information Loss
While transforming the spectral shape yields undeniable benefits, it does not commute freely with arbitrary stretching of the starting base ($\theta$). Through systemic ablations down to 50M parameter scales, we recorded specific failure modes when base frequencies were excessively amplified independently of the context prior. For instance, testing a geometric base of $300,000$ alongside shape reformulation occasionally yielded perplexities mathematically *worse* (-9.1% relative) than the rigid standard baseline. This corroborates our theoretical prediction: arbitrarily compressing the high-frequency spectrum cannibalizes spatial Fisher Information. Unless precisely anchoring the highest frequencies (`rigid_j0`), intermediate grammatical structures are irreversibly "starved", leading to catastrophic failures in precise needle-retrieval evaluations, despite deceptively low overall perplexities.

## 3. Implementation Mirages in Length Extension
A critical methodological discussion raised by this work concerns the "Implementation Mirages" prevalent in the length extension literature. When porting our strategies to the 8B parameter scale, early experiments showed dramatic (yet entirely artificial) performance chasms between baseline Position Interpolation (PI), YaRN, and our early Hybrid proposals. Deep inspection revealed that mixing framework-level scaling APIs (like HuggingFace's `rope_scaling=linear`) against customized attention forward-pass monkey-patches generated unequal under-the-hood kernel executions (e.g., disabling Flash Attention or corrupting arithmetic precision). 

To ensure our metrics reflect mathematical reality rather than engineering artifacts, we migrated all experiments to a strict `inv_freq` tensor injection protocol. We strongly advise the community to view any comparative length-extension claims that rely on diverging backend implementations with intense skepticism.

## 4. Future Work
While we mathematically characterize the connection between the distance prior $D(\Delta)$ and the optimal allocation density, determining the real-time empirical probability prior for a given dynamic sequence without multi-pass overhead remains computationally intractable. Future work should explore continuous context-aware dynamic frequency allocation, potentially utilizing the initial layers as dynamic phase estimators to inject sequence-specific priors into the deeper attention blocks.

## 5. From "Best Method" to "Warning Boundary"
Figure 3 in the main text should be interpreted as a warning diagnostic, not a leaderboard plot. The two-panel comparison (`rho(phi)` theoretical band vs actual warp; corrected `E_diag(phi)` trend) shows that aggressive sigmoid-like warps can move outside the bounded-amplitude regime predicted by Theorem 2 and therefore increase waterbed risk described by Theorem 3. In contrast, anchored-sigmoid stays closer to the admissible region by preserving high-frequency anchors while redistributing lower frequencies smoothly.

This framing also aligns with our statistical reporting discipline: the current 8B fair LongBench comparisons in Table 4 show trend-level gains but are not statistically significant under the current sample size (`p > 0.05` across key pairwise tests). We therefore claim predictive warning value and mechanism-level guidance, rather than unconditional superiority.

From a theory standpoint, this is consistent with the regularized inverse-problem view in Section 3.6: interference minimization alone is incomplete; a logarithmic local-resolution barrier is required to prevent frequency starvation and to explain why mild/unified-base regimes may favor near-uniform allocations.
